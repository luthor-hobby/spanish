Placeres Prohibidos

Laurell K. Hamilton · Capítulo 6: Seis

Lectura glosada para el estudio del español — cada oración con glosa, vocabulario y notas gramaticales

Contexto del capítulo

Anita vuelve al Placeres Prohibidos a recoger a Catherine y a Mónica. En la puerta se encuentra con Jean-Claude en un estado de ánimo inquietante. Dentro, descubre que Catherine está en trance en el escenario, bajo el control de un vampiro antiguo llamado Aubrey. Lo que sigue es el capítulo más peligroso hasta ahora: Anita se enfrenta sola a uno de los vampiros más poderosos que ha conocido.

§ De vuelta al umbral
01
Llegué al Placeres Prohibidos poco después de la medianoche. Jean-Claude estaba al pie de los escalones, apoyado en la pared, completamente inmóvil. No pude ver si respiraba. El viento le agitaba el encaje de la camisa, y un rizo de pelo negro surcaba la tersa palidez de su mejilla.
I arrived back at Guilty Pleasures a little after midnight. Jean-Claude was standing at the bottom of the steps. He was leaning against the wall, utterly still. If he was breathing, I couldn't see it. The wind blew the lace on his shirt. A lock of black hair trailed across the smooth paleness of his cheek.

gramática
  • no pude ver si respiraba — Ver + si + imperfecto: perception + indirect yes/no question. 'I couldn't tell whether he was breathing.' The imperfect (respiraba) frames breathing as an ongoing state she could not confirm.
  • le agitaba el encaje — Dative of interest le + object the wind acts on (el encaje). The wind stirs the lace that is on/near him. Standard Spanish construction for external agents acting on something belonging to a person.
  • surcaba la tersa palidez — Surcar: to furrow, to cut across (used for ships crossing water, ploughs crossing earth). Applied here to a lock of hair crossing the cheek — a deliberately elevated, literary verb choice that sets the scene's tone.
02 💬 Jean-Claude / Anita
diálogo
—Hueles a sangre ajena, ma petite. —Nadie que conocieras —le dije con una sonrisa encantadora. —¿Has matado a algún vampiro, mi pequeña reanimadora? —La voz, baja y sombría, llena de rabia contenida, me recorrió la piel como un viento frío. —No —susurré con la voz enronquecida. No lo había oído hablar nunca de aquel modo. —Te llaman la Ejecutora, ¿lo sabías? —Sí —dije. No había hecho nada amenazador, pero en aquel momento no habría pasado junto a él ni loca. Por mí, como si tapiaban la puerta.
"You smell of other people's blood, ma petite." I smiled at him, sweetly. "It was no one you knew." His voice when it came was low and dark, full of a quiet rage. It slithered across my skin, like a cold wind. "Have you been killing vampires, my little animator?" "No." I whispered it, my voice suddenly hoarse. I had never heard his voice like that. "They call you The Executioner, did you know that?" "Yes." He had done nothing to threaten me, yet nothing at that moment would have forced me to pass him. They might as well have barred the door.

gramática
  • nadie que conocieras — Nadie que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: indefinite negative antecedent. 'No one that you would know.' The subjunctive follows nadie que because the person is hypothetical/unidentified. Contrast: nadie que conocía would imply a specific person she actually knew.
  • no habría pasado junto a él ni loca — Ni + adjective/adverb in emphatic negation: 'not even if she were crazy'. The conditional perfect (habría pasado) is the hypothetical she refuses. Ni loca (not even crazy) = not for anything in the world. Idiomatic and very forceful.
  • por mí, como si tapiaban la puerta — Note the indicative tapiaban after como si. Standard grammar requires subjunctive, but colloquial Spanish sometimes uses indicative with como si for a vivid, matter-of-fact effect. Here: 'as far as I was concerned, they had effectively walled up the door.' The indicative makes it feel concrete rather than hypothetical.
03 💬 Jean-Claude / Anita
diálogo

No sabía muy bien de qué iba Jean-Claude, pero no tenía intención de mentirle. —Catorce —contesté. —Y tú nos llamas asesinos. Me quedé mirándolo sin saber qué pretendía que dijera.
"How many kills do you have to your credit?" I didn't like this conversation. It wasn't going to end anywhere I wanted to be. I knew one master vampire who could smell lies. I didn't understand Jean-Claude's mood, but I wasn't about to lie to him. "Fourteen." "And you call us murderers." I just stared at him, not sure what he wanted me to say.

gramática
  • tenía pinta de acabar mal — Tener pinta de + infinitivo: 'to look like it's going to'. Pinta = appearance, look. Colloquial: tiene pinta de ser difícil (it looks difficult). Not to be confused with pinta as a unit of measure.
  • no sabía muy bien de qué iba — Ir de: colloquial for 'to be about, to concern'. 'She didn't really know what Jean-Claude was playing at / what his deal was.' Informal register; the indefiniteness of his mood is captured by the vague de qué iba.
  • sin saber qué pretendía que dijera — Pretender que + subjuntivo: to expect/want that. Dijera: imperfect subjunctive of decir. Note again: pretender ≠ English 'pretend'. He wasn't faking — he was genuinely expecting something from her.

diálogo

"Yes, thank you, master." The master vampire smiled. "I've told you before, Buzz, don't call me master." "Yes, M-M... Jean-Claude." The vampire gave his wondrous, nearly touchable laugh. gramática
  • te tengo dicho que no me llames amo — Tener + participio: resultative state. 'I have told you (and the telling stands as a standing instruction).' More insistent than te he dicho: the participle agrees with the implicit object and implies the instruction is still in force.
  • dentro se está más caliente — Se estar impersonal for environmental conditions: 'it is warmer inside'. Compare: aquí se está bien (it's nice here). The se makes it impersonal — no specific subject experiences the warmth.
  • y ya puestos, tampoco sabía de qué habíamos estado hablando — Ya puestos: 'while we're at it / for that matter'. Idiomatic filler: since she's already admitting one thing she doesn't understand, she might as well admit the other. Pluperfect progressive (habíamos estado hablando): what they had been talking about in the preceding minutes.

diálogo

gramática
  • ¿sería mucho abusar pedirte que entraras y les dijeras? — Sería mucho abusar: 'would it be too much to ask?' Conditional for polite softening. Pedirte que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: indirect request. Two coordinated subjunctives: entraras and dijeras.
  • lo de mostrar los colmillos es típico de los muertos recientes — Lo de + infinitivo/noun: 'the business/habit of'. Anita's dry taxonomic aside about vampire behaviour. Lo de nominalises the whole concept of fang-flashing into a category she can label.
  • sabía que dirías algo así — Reported speech in past: saber que + condicional simple. 'I knew you would say something like that.' The conditional replaces the future in reported past speech: she had predicted (past) that he would say (future from that past point) this.
§ Catherine en trance
06
Entré en la penumbra del local y me encontré a la chica de la consigna esperando. Le entregué el crucifijo, y ella me dio un resguardo. No era un intercambio justo. Jean-Claude no estaba por ninguna parte. Catherine estaba en el escenario. De pie, inmóvil y con los ojos muy abiertos, tenía la expresión inocente y desvalida, como de niña, característica de los que duermen; su larga melena cobriza relucía bajo los focos. Sabía reconocer un estado de trance en cuanto lo veía.
I went into the twilit dark of the club. The holy item check girl was waiting for me at the door. I gave her my cross. She gave me a check stub. It wasn't a fair trade. Jean-Claude was nowhere in sight. Catherine was on the stage. She was standing motionless, eyes wide. Her face had that open, fragile look that faces get when they sleep, like a child's face. Her long, copper-colored hair glistened in the lights. I knew a deep trance when I saw it.

gramática
  • no estaba por ninguna parte — No estar por ninguna parte: 'to be nowhere'. Double negation: no + ninguna. Stronger than no estaba en ningún sitio. Por ninguna parte = in no direction/place (implies she looked everywhere).
  • tenía la expresión inocente y desvalida, como de niña, característica de los que duermen — Extended appositive noun phrase. Three stacked descriptors — adjectives, comparison, relative clause — all modifying expresión. Anita pauses to observe carefully before acting; the detail of the child-face makes the threat feel worse.
  • sabía reconocer un estado de trance en cuanto lo veía — Saber + infinitivo: to know how to. En cuanto + imperfecto: 'as soon as she saw it' (habitual past). The imperfect signals professional experience — she has seen trances many times before.

gramática
  • me dedicó una sonrisa cínica y desagradable — Dedicar + expression: to give someone a look, a smile, a gesture. More deliberate than me sonrió: Monica directed the smile at her as an act. The adjectives cínica y desagradable confirm she knows exactly what's about to happen to Anita.
  • no salió de detrás del telón; sencillamente, apareció detrás de ella — Contrast structure: no X; Y. The semicolon separates the negated expectation from the impossible reality. Sencillamente (simply, just) is deliberately understated — there was nothing simple about it.
  • entonces comprendí qué veían los humanos — Entonces as a moment marker: 'that was when'. Comprendí (preterite): sudden, complete realisation. Qué veían: indirect question, imperfect — what humans habitually perceive (not what they saw in that moment).
08 💬 vampiro / Anita
diálogo

Su voz me sonó casi vulgar después de haberle visto la cara. —Llámala —me ordenó. Abrí los ojos y vi que el público me observaba. Estaba viva; pero ¿hasta qué punto? El vampiro la había sumido en un trance muy profundo. Aquello quería decir que podría llamarla en cualquier momento, desde cualquier lugar, y que ella acudiría obediente. Desde aquel mismo momento, la vida de Catherine le pertenecía.
The vampire stared at me. His hair was golden silk, his skin ivory, eyes like drowning pools. I closed my eyes and shook my head. This couldn't be happening. No one was that beautiful. His voice was almost ordinary after the face, but it was a command. "Call her." I opened my eyes to find the audience staring at me. I glanced at Catherine's blank face and knew what would happen, but like any ignorant client I had to try. "Catherine, Catherine, can you hear me?" She never moved; only the faintest of movements showed her breathing. She was alive, but for how long? The vampire had gotten to her, deep trance. That meant he could call her anytime, anywhere, and she would come. From this moment on, her life belonged to him.

gramática
  • su pelo parecía de seda dorada; su piel, de marfil — Elliptical parallel construction: the second clause omits the verb (parecía) because it's identical. The semicolon separates the two parallel comparisons. A literary technique for rapid, stacked description.
  • su voz me sonó casi vulgar después de haberle visto la cara — Sonarle + adjective: to seem, to sound to someone. Después de haber + participio: after having done (perfect infinitive). The face is so extraordinary that the voice is diminished by comparison.
  • aquello quería decir que podría llamarla en cualquier momento — Querer decir que: to mean that. Anita explicitly states the implication — the narrative slows for the reader to fully understand the threat. Podría llamarla: conditional for a new, permanent capability.

§ Aubrey
10 💬 Aubrey / Anita
diálogo
Yo ya sabía que tendría problemas. El vampiro del escenario me estaba mirando, y notaba el peso de su mirada en la piel. Sentía las pulsaciones de su voluntad, su poder, su personalidad o lo que fuera. Las sentía como si fueran ráfagas de viento. Me ponían la piel de gallina. —Me llamo Aubrey —dijo el vampiro—. ¿Y tú? La boca se me había secado de repente, pero mi nombre carecía de importancia. Podía decírselo. —Anita. —Anita. Precioso. Las rodillas se negaban a sostenerme, y me senté en la silla.
I already knew I was in trouble. The vampire on the stage was staring at me. It was like weight against my skin. His will, force, personality, whatever it was, beat against me. I could feel it like a pulsing wind. The skin on my arms crawled with it. "I am Aubrey," the vampire said. "Give me your name." My mouth was suddenly dry, but my name was not important. He could have that. "Anita." "Anita. How pretty." My knees sort of buckled and spilled me into a chair.

gramática
  • su voluntad, su poder, su personalidad o lo que fuera — Escalating list with an open-ended o lo que fuera. Imperfect subjunctive (fuera) in a free-standing clause: 'or whatever it might be'. The vagueness is deliberate — Anita can't name what she's feeling.
  • la boca se me había secado de repente — Body-reaction construction: dative me + body part subject. Pluperfect: the dryness had set in before she speaks. Secarse (reflexive) = to go dry. The mouth dried on her, not something she did.
  • las rodillas se negaban a sostenerme — Negarse a + infinitivo: to refuse to. Here the rodillas (knees) are given agency — they refuse. A vivid personification of physical collapse. The verb choice dramatises involuntary loss of control.
11 💬 Aubrey / Anita (narración)
—Ven, Anita, sube conmigo al escenario. No tenía ni de lejos el puntazo seductor de la voz de Jean-Claude. Le faltaba textura, pero jamás había sentido nada parecido a la mente que había detrás de la voz. Era antigua, antiquísima. Su poder me dolía en los huesos. —Ven. Yo negué con la cabeza, una y otra vez. No era capaz de hacer nada más. No lograba articular palabra ni pensar con coherencia, pero tenía muy claro que no podía levantarme de la silla. Si me acercaba a él, me tendría en su poder, igual que a Catherine. El sudor me empapaba la espalda.
"Come, Anita, join me on the stage." His voice wasn't as good as Jean-Claude's, it just wasn't. There was no texture to it, but the mind behind the voice was like nothing I had ever felt. It was ancient, terribly ancient. The force of his mind made my bones ache. "Come." I kept shaking my head, over and over. It was all I could do. No words, no real thoughts, but I knew I could not get out of this chair. If I came to him now, he would have power over me just as he did Catherine. Sweat soaked through the back of my blouse.

gramática
  • no tenía ni de lejos el puntazo seductor de la voz de Jean-Claude — Ni de lejos: 'not even remotely, nowhere near'. Strong emphatic negation. El puntazo: colloquial noun from punto + augmentative -azo = 'the great punch/quality'. Anita's professional rating system for vampire vocal powers.
  • antigua, antiquísima — Adjective followed immediately by its absolute superlative for intensification. Antiquísima is the irregular superlative of antigua (antiguo → antiquísimo). The doubling mimics the sensation of weight — the word itself becomes heavier.
  • si me acercaba a él, me tendría en su poder — Si + imperfecto de indicativo, condicional: real condition seen from a past vantage point. 'If she were to approach, he would have power over her.' The imperfect (me acercaba) signals the condition is being contemplated in the present of the narration, not hypothetically removed.
§ La resistencia
12 💬 Aubrey / camarero vampiro / Anita
diálogo

Jean-Claude le tendió la otra mano a Aubrey. —No temas —me susurró cuando traté de apartarme—, te protegeré. Lo prometo. No sé por qué, pero lo creí; quizá porque no tenía a nadie más en quien creer. Nos arrastró a Aubrey y a mí a proscenio. —Esperamos que hayan disfrutado de nuestra modesta representación —dijo con una voz sensual que acarició al público—. Ha sido muy realista, ¿verdad? El público se agitó incómodo; las caras mostraban miedo. Jean-Claude sonrió y le soltó la mano a Aubrey.
Jean-Claude held his other hand out to Aubrey. "No fear, I will protect you, I swear it." I tried to pull away, and he whispered. I believed him, I don't know why, maybe because I had no one else to believe. He led Aubrey and me to the front of the stage. His rich voice caressed the crowd. "We hope you enjoyed our little melodrama. It was very realistic, wasn't it?" The audience shifted uncomfortably, fear plain in their faces. He smiled out at them and dropped Aubrey's hand.

gramática
  • quizá porque no tenía a nadie más en quien creer — Nadie en quien creer: 'no one to believe in'. En quien: relative pronoun with preposition retained before it — in contrast to English 'no one to believe in' which strands the preposition. The reasoning is brutal: she trusts Jean-Claude by elimination.
  • esperamos que hayan disfrutado — Esperar que + subjuntivo: hope that. Present perfect subjunctive (hayan disfrutado): he hopes they have enjoyed the completed performance. Jean-Claude's theatrical MC voice — smooth, inclusive, performing normalcy over what just happened.
  • una voz sensual que acarició al público — Acariciar: to caress, to stroke. Personal a before el público (treated as animate, as an individual to be touched). The voice caresses the crowd as a physical act — Jean-Claude's power weaponised for crowd management.

Me desabrochó el puño y me subió la manga, para dejar al descubierto la quemadura. La marca oscura de la cruz resaltaba en la piel. El público seguía callado, sin comprender. Jean-Claude se apartó el encaje del pecho y mostró su quemadura en forma de cruz. Hubo un momento de estupefacción y silencio; después estallaron los aplausos. A nuestro alrededor rugían los gritos y los silbidos. Me habían tomado por vampira, y creían que todo había sido un número. He unbuttoned my sleeve and pushed it back, exposing the burn scar. The cross was dark against my skin. The audience was silent, still not understanding. Jean-Claude pulled the lace away from his chest, exposing his own cross-shaped burn. There was a moment of stunned silence, then applause thundered around the room. Screams and shouts, and whistles roared around us. They thought I was a vampire, and it had all been an act. I stared at Jean-Claude's smiling face and the matching scars: his chest, my arm.

gramática
  • me desabrochó el puño y me subió la manga — Two actions with dative of interest me. Jean-Claude acts on Anita's sleeve (el puño, la manga) — her body is the indirect object of his theatrical gesture.
  • me habían tomado por vampira — Tomar a alguien por: to mistake someone for. Pluperfect: the misidentification happened over the course of the performance. No article before vampira (predicate noun after tomar por).
  • las dos quemaduras; en su pecho, en mi brazo — Nominal fragments after the semicolon: 'his chest, my arm'. The two scars are stated without verb — the ellipsis enacts the mirroring. Jean-Claude and Anita are linked, whether she wants to be or not.

diálogo
Jean-Claude tiró de mí hacia abajo para que me agachara, y me obligó a saludar al público. —Tenemos que hablar, Anita —susurró cuando el aplauso empezó a decaer por fin—. La vida de tu amiga Catherine depende de lo que hagas. —Maté a los monstruos que me dejaron esta cicatriz —dije, mirándolo a los ojos. Me dedicó una amplia sonrisa sin mostrar apenas los colmillos. —Qué encantadora coincidencia. Yo también.
Jean-Claude's hand pulled me down into a bow. As the applause finally began to fade, Jean-Claude whispered, "We need to talk, Anita. Your friend Catherine's life depends on your actions." I met his eyes and said, "I killed the things that gave me this scar." He smiled broadly, showing just a hint of fang. "What a lovely coincidence. So did I."

gramática
  • tiró de mí hacia abajo para que me agachara — Tirar de: to pull (someone/something). Para que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: purpose with different subjects. He pulled (his action) so that she would bow (her action). The subjunctive is obligatory when subjects differ.
  • la vida de tu amiga Catherine depende de lo que hagas — Depender de lo que + subjuntivo: 'depends on what you do'. Lo que hagas: present subjunctive in an indirect question, because what she will do is unknown and open. Jean-Claude drops the bombshell under cover of applause.
  • qué encantadora coincidencia. Yo también. — Jean-Claude matches her revelation with his own in the final four words of the chapter. Yo también: 'so did I / me too'. The grammatical echo mirrors the thematic echo: their scars, their kills, their shared history. The chapter closes with a door opening.
