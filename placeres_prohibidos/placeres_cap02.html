Placeres Prohibidos

Laurell K. Hamilton · Capítulo 2: Dos

Lectura glosada para el estudio del español — cada oración con glosa, vocabulario y notas gramaticales

Contexto del capítulo

Anita llega a casa al amanecer después de trabajar toda la noche. Apenas ha conseguido dormirse cuando suena el teléfono: es Mónica Vespucci, compañera de trabajo de su amiga Catherine, que la recluta como conductora designada para una despedida de soltera sorpresa esa misma noche. El capítulo presenta el piso y la rutina de Anita, su carácter irónico, y termina con un sueño inquietante que enlaza con el funeral de Willie McCoy.

§ El amanecer
01
Aquella mañana vi salir el sol mientras volvía a casa. Odio el amanecer.
I had gotten to see the sun rise as I drove home that morning. I hate sunrises.
vocabulario
aquella mañanathat morning (distant past; aquella vs. esta)
vi salir el solI saw/watched the sun rise
mientras volvíawhile I was driving / on my way back
el amanecerdawn, sunrise
gramática
  • vi salir el sol — Verbo de percepción + infinitivo: ver, oír, sentir + infinitive express witnessing an action in progress. Compare: vi salir (I saw [it] rise — the whole event) vs. vi saliendo (I saw it rising — mid-action).
  • mientras volvía — Imperfecto for the background, ongoing action (driving home) during which the main event (seeing the sunrise) occurred. The preterite vi cuts across the imperfect background.
02
Significa que me he pasado con el horario y he trabajado toda la puta noche.
They mean I've overscheduled myself and worked all bloody night.
vocabulario
significa queit means that
me he pasado con el horarioI've overscheduled myself / overdone it with timing
toda la puta nochethe whole bloody/damn night (puta: vulgar intensifier)
gramática
  • me he pasado con — Pasarse con algo: reflexive idiom meaning to overdo or go too far with something. Me he pasado con la sal = I've overdone the salt. Here: overbooked herself.
  • puta as intensifier — In colloquial Castilian Spanish, puta functions as a strong intensifier comparable to English 'bloody' or 'damn'. Vulgar but extremely common in informal speech.
03
San Luis tiene más árboles en los márgenes de las carreteras que ninguna otra ciudad por la que haya conducido. Casi estaba dispuesta a reconocer que, bañados por los primeros rayos del alba, los árboles eran bonitos. Casi.
St. Louis has more trees edging its highways than any other city I have driven through. I could almost admit the trees looked nice in the first light of dawn, almost.
vocabulario
los márgenesthe edges, borders
ninguna otra ciudadno other city / any other city (negative context)
haya conducidohave driven (present perfect subjunctive)
dispuesta a reconocerwilling to admit
bañados porbathed in, lit by
los primeros rayos del albathe first rays of dawn
gramática
  • por la que haya conducido — Subjuntivo en cláusula relativa: when the antecedent is negated or non-specific, Spanish uses the subjunctive. Ninguna ciudad por la que haya conducido = no city through which I have (ever) driven. Compare the indicative: la ciudad por la que condujo (the specific city she drove through).
  • bañados por — Participio como adjetivo: bañar (to bathe/wash) used as a past participle modifying los árboles, agreeing in gender and number. Common literary construction for describing light or atmosphere.
  • Casi. — Sentence fragment for emphasis. The repetition of casi (the English 'almost, almost') underscores Anita's reluctance to concede beauty. A classic Hamilton dry-humour beat.
§ El piso
04
Mi piso tiene siempre un aspecto tan luminoso y alegre al sol de la mañana que resulta deprimente. Las paredes son del mismo color de helado de vainilla que las de cualquier otro piso que haya visto.
My apartment always looks depressingly white and cheerful in morning sunlight. The walls are the same vanilla ice cream white as every apartment I've ever seen.
vocabulario
el pisoflat, apartment (Spain); floor in LatAm
un aspectoan appearance, a look
luminoso y alegrebright and cheerful
resulta deprimenteturns out to be / comes across as depressing
helado de vainillavanilla ice cream
gramática
  • tan… que — Consecutiva de intensidad: tan + adjective + que = so… that. Tan luminoso que resulta deprimente = so bright that it's depressing.
  • resultar + adjetivo — Resultar as a linking verb (like ser/estar/parecer): it expresses the result or impression something makes. More formal/literary than es deprimente; implies the outcome of assessment.
  • que haya visto — Again the subjuntivo en relativa with a universal/non-specific antecedent (cualquier otro piso). Cualquier + noun consistently triggers subjunctive in relative clauses.
05
La moqueta es de un gris pasable, muchísimo mejor que la típica marrón caca de perro. Es un piso espacioso, con un dormitorio.
The carpeting is a nice shade of grey, preferable to that dog poop brown that is more common. The apartment is a roomy one-bedroom.
vocabulario
la moquetafitted carpet (Spain only; LatAm uses alfombra)
pasablepassable, acceptable
muchísimo mejorfar better (-ísimo superlative + comparative)
marrón caca de perrodog-poop brown (Anita's colourful register)
espaciosospacious, roomy
un dormitorioone bedroom
gramática
  • muchísimo mejor — Stacked intensification: mucho + -ísimo (absolute superlative) + mejor (comparative). Spanish allows this layering for emphasis; English needs 'far/much/so much better'.
  • la típica marrón caca de perro — Noun phrase used as colour adjective. Colour adjectives in Spanish are invariable when they are nouns (marrón) or noun phrases. The whole phrase marrón caca de perro modifies an implied moqueta.
06
Tiene vistas al parque de al lado, dicen, pero es como si tuviera vistas a Marte. Por mí, no harían falta ventanas; me las apaño con unas cortinas tupidas que convierten el día más luminoso en una agradable penumbra.
I am told it has a nice view of the park next door. You couldn't prove it by me. If I had my choice, there would be no windows. I get by with heavy drapes that turn the brightest day to cool twilight.
vocabulario
vistas al parquea view of the park
de al ladonext door, nearby
dicenthey say / supposedly
es como si + subjuntivoit's as if…
por mías far as I'm concerned / for my part
no harían faltawouldn't be needed (conditional of hacer falta)
me las apañoI manage, I get by (colloquial)
cortinas tupidasheavy, thick curtains
penumbrahalf-light, twilight, dim light
gramática
  • es como si tuviera — Como si + imperfecto de subjuntivo: 'as if' always takes imperfect subjunctive in Spanish, expressing a hypothetical comparison. Es como si tuviera vistas a Marte = It's as if she had a view of Mars.
  • por mí, no harían falta — Condicional expressing a personal hypothetical. Hacer falta = to be needed/necessary. Por mí introduces the personal standpoint: 'for all I care / left to me'.
  • me las apaño — Apañarse: Castilian colloquial verb meaning to manage or get by. The las is an expletive pronoun (no clear referent) that intensifies the reflexive feel. Equivalent: me las arreglo. Very Spain-specific.
07
Encendí la radio para ahogar el ruido de mis vecinos de hábitos diurnos. El sueño se apoderó de mí con la suave música de Chopin.
I switched the radio on low to drown the small noises of my day-living neighbors. Sleep sucked me under to the soft music of Chopin.
vocabulario
encendíI switched on / turned on (pretérito of encender)
ahogarto drown out, to muffle
vecinos de hábitos diurnosday-schedule neighbours (i.e., normal people)
el sueño se apoderó de mísleep took hold of me / swept over me
apoderarse deto take hold of, to seize
suavesoft, gentle
gramática
  • para ahogar — Para + infinitivo expressing purpose: 'in order to drown out'. Contrast with por + infinitive, which expresses reason/cause rather than purpose.
  • el sueño se apoderó de mí — Apoderarse de is a pronominal verb (always reflexive). The subject here is el sueño (sleep); Anita is the object (de mí). This reversal — sleep as the agent acting on her — creates a vivid, literary image.
§ El teléfono
08
Y muy poco después sonó el teléfono. Me quedé tumbada un instante, odiándome por no haber conectado el contestador. Si no hacía caso, con un poco de suerte… Al quinto timbrazo me di por vencida.
A minute later the phone rang. I lay there for a minute, cursing myself for forgetting to turn on the answering machine. Maybe if I ignored it? Five rings later I gave in.
vocabulario
muy poco despuésa short while later, moments later
sonórang (pretérito of sonar)
me quedé tumbadaI lay there / I stayed lying down
odiándomehating myself (gerundio)
el contestadoranswering machine
hacer casoto pay attention, to heed
el timbrazoring (of phone/bell)
me di por vencidaI gave up / gave in
gramática
  • me quedé tumbada — Quedarse + participio: expresses remaining in a state. Tumbada (fem.) agrees with Anita. Compare: se quedó dormido (he fell asleep / stayed asleep), se quedó callada (she stayed quiet).
  • odiándome por no haber conectado — Gerundio as simultaneous action + por + infinitivo compuesto (perfect infinitive) for the reason. No haber conectado = not having turned on (completed action prior to the lying there).
  • me di por vencida — Fixed phrase: darse por vencido/a = to give up, to concede defeat. Gender-marked: vencida because Anita is female. Equivalent to rendirse but more idiomatic.
§ La llamada de Mónica
09 💬 Anita / Mónica
diálogo
—¿Sí? —Oh, lo siento. ¿Te he despertado? Era una voz de mujer que no me sonaba de nada. Como pretendiera venderme algo, se iba a enterar.
"Hello." "Oh, I'm sorry. Did I wake you?" It was a woman I didn't know. If it was a salesperson I was going to become violent.
vocabulario
¿Sí?Hello? (answering the phone in Spain)
¿te he despertado?Did I wake you? (present perfect)
no me sonaba de nadadidn't ring a bell at all
como pretendieraif she was trying to / should she try to (como si + subj.)
se iba a enterarshe was going to hear about it / she'd be sorry
gramática
  • ¿Sí? — Standard phone greeting in Spain (alongside ¿Dígame? or ¿Diga?). LatAm uses ¿Aló?, ¿Bueno?, or ¿Hola? instead.
  • sonar de nada / sonar de algo — Sonarle a alguien: to ring a bell, to seem familiar. No me suena de nada = it doesn't ring any bell. Common in Castilian Spanish.
  • como pretendiera — Como + imperfecto de subjuntivo: conditional 'if' with a threatening or hypothetical nuance. More vivid than si pretendía; implies 'should she dare to try'.
  • se iba a enterar — Ir a + infinitivo in imperfecto: near-future in the past. Enterarse here is idiomatic: 'to hear about it / to get what's coming'. The phrase ¡te vas a enterar! is a common threat.
10 💬 Anita
diálogo
—¿Quién es? —pregunté, parpadeando para enfocar el reloj de la mesilla. Eran las ocho. Había dormido casi dos horas. Alegría.
"Who is this?" I blinked at the bedside clock. It was eight. I'd had nearly two hours of sleep. Yippee.
vocabulario
parpadeandoblinking (gerundio of parpadear)
enfocarto focus
la mesilla (de noche)bedside table, nightstand
eran las ochoit was eight o'clock
había dormidoI had slept (pluperfect)
Alegría.Joy. / Yippee. (heavy sarcasm)
gramática
  • parpadeando para enfocar — Gerundio expressing manner/means + para + infinitivo for purpose. 'Blinking in order to focus.' The gerundio describes how she asked the question.
  • Eran las ocho — Imperfecto for clock time: always era/eran in reported or narrative time, not preterite. Eran (plural) because las ocho is plural; exception: era la una.
  • Alegría. — Ironic one-word sentence. No verb needed; the noun alone carries the sarcastic tone. Hamilton uses these punchy fragments throughout to mimic Anita's interior monologue rhythm.
11 💬 Mónica / Anita
diálogo
—Soy Mónica Vespucci —dijo, como si aquello lo explicara todo. Pues no. —¿Sí? —Intenté decirlo con tono amable, para animarla a continuar, pero creo que me salió algo parecido a un gruñido.
"I'm Monica Vespucci." She said it like it should explain everything. It didn't. "Yes." I tried to sound helpful, encouraging. I think it came out as a growl.
vocabulario
como si aquello lo explicara todoas if that explained everything
Pues no.Well, it didn't. / Nope.
con tono amablein a friendly/pleasant tone
animarla a continuarto encourage her to go on
me salióit came out (as)
un gruñidoa growl, a grunt
gramática
  • como si… lo explicara — Como si + imperfecto de subjuntivo (again): hypothetical comparison. The lo is a direct object pronoun anticipating todo (left-dislocated). Explicara is the -ra imperfect subjunctive form of explicar.
  • Pues no. — Pues as a filler/connector. Here it introduces a blunt contradiction. Colloquial and very Spanish; equivalent to 'Well, no' or simply 'Nope'.
  • me salió algo parecido a un gruñido — Salir used reflexively/impersonally: the sound 'came out' of her. Algo parecido a = something resembling. Anita distances herself from her own rudeness with comic understatement.
12 💬 Mónica / Anita (narración)
diálogo
—Oh, vaya. Soy la Mónica que trabaja con Catherine Maison. Me acurruqué, con el auricular en la mano, y traté de pensar. No se me da muy bien cuando sólo he dormido dos horas.
"Oh, my, uh. I'm the Monica that works with Catherine Maison." I huddled around the receiver and tried to think. I don't think really well on two hours of sleep.
vocabulario
vayaoh my / well / gosh (filler expression)
me acurruquéI curled up / huddled (pretérito of acurrucarse)
el auricularthe receiver, the handset
traté de pensarI tried to think
no se me da bienI'm not good at it / I don't do it well
gramática
  • vaya — Multi-purpose exclamation. Can express surprise, dismay, or serve as a filler. In ¡Vaya coche! it's emphatic ('What a car!'). Here it's a hesitation marker. Do not confuse with vaya as present subjunctive of ir.
  • no se me da bien — Dársele bien/mal algo a alguien: idiomatic for being good or bad at something. Se me da bien cocinar = I'm good at cooking. The construction is impersonal + dative: the skill is what 'gives itself well' to the person.
13
El nombre de Catherine sí que me sonaba: era el de una buena amiga. Es posible que me hubiera mencionado a aquella mujer, pero no habría podido identificarla aunque me fuera la vida en ello.
Catherine was a good friend, a name I knew. She had probably mentioned this woman to me, but for the life of me, I couldn't place her.
vocabulario
sí que me sonabathat name did ring a bell (sí que = emphatic affirmation)
es posible que + subjuntivoit's possible that…
me hubiera mencionadohad mentioned to me (pluperfect subjunctive)
no habría podido identificarlaI wouldn't have been able to place her (conditional perfect)
aunque me fuera la vida en elloeven if my life depended on it
gramática
  • sí que — Emphatic affirmation particle. Sí que me sonaba = 'that name definitely rang a bell' (contrasting with the unknown Monica). Very common in Spain to stress a positive against an implicit negative.
  • es posible que me hubiera mencionado — Expresión de posibilidad + pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo: the matrix verb is present (es posible) but the subordinate action is past-before-past, requiring the pluperfect subjunctive.
  • aunque me fuera la vida en ello — Aunque + imperfecto de subjuntivo: concessive clause expressing a hypothetical. 'Even if her life were at stake.' Irle a alguien la vida en algo = to have one's life depend on something. Fixed expression.
14 💬 Anita
diálogo
—Claro, Mónica, sí. ¿Qué quieres? —Mi voz me sonó desagradable hasta a mí—. Y lo siento si tengo la voz rara, pero es que he estado trabajando hasta las seis.
"Sure, Monica, yes. What do you want?" It sounded rude, even to me. "I'm sorry if I don't sound too good. I got off work at six."
vocabulario
mi voz me sonó desagradablemy voice sounded unpleasant even to me
hasta a míeven to me
la voz raraa strange/off voice
es quethe thing is / it's because (explanatory filler)
he estado trabajando hasta las seisI've been working until six
gramática
  • mi voz me sonó desagradable — Sonar + adjetivo: to sound (a certain way). Reflexive-ish dative: me = to me. The voice is the subject; Anita is the experiencer.
  • es que — Discourse marker introducing an explanation or excuse. Untranslatable literally; functions like 'the thing is' or 'it's just that'. Ubiquitous in spoken Spanish.
  • he estado trabajando — Pretérito perfecto compuesto + gerundio (present perfect progressive): emphasises the duration of a recently completed action. Preferred in Spain over the simple preterite for recent events.
15 💬 Mónica / Anita (narración)
diálogo
—¿Sólo has dormido dos horas? Dios mío. Me querrás matar. Me abstuve de contestar. No soy tan maleducada.
"My god, you mean you've only had two hours of sleep. Do you want to shoot me, or what?" I didn't answer the question. I'm not that rude.
vocabulario
Dios míoMy God / Oh my goodness
me querrás mataryou must want to kill me (future of supposition)
me abstuve deI refrained from, I held back from
contestarto answer, to reply
maleducadarude, ill-mannered
gramática
  • me querrás matar — Futuro de probabilidad/suposición: the future tense in Spanish can express a reasonable assumption about the present. Querrás = 'you must want / you probably want'. Compare: ¿Dónde estará? = 'Where can he be?'
  • me abstuve de contestar — Abstenerse de + infinitivo: to refrain from doing something. Formal/literary register; in speech you'd more likely hear no dije nada or me callé.
16 💬 Anita / Mónica
diálogo
—¿Querías algo, Mónica? —Sí, claro. Estoy organizando la despedida de soltera de Catherine, pero es una fiesta sorpresa. Ya sabes que se casa el mes que viene.
"Did you want something, Monica?" "Sure, yes. I'm throwing a surprise bachelorette party for Catherine. You know she gets married next month."
vocabulario

una fiesta sorpresaa surprise party

gramática
  • ¿Querías algo? — Imperfecto as a politeness strategy: using the imperfect instead of the present softens the question. 'Did you want something?' is more patient than '¿Quieres algo?' Compare: ¿Qué deseaba? (What did you wish? — shop assistant register).
  • se casa el mes que viene — Presente con valor de futuro próximo: the present tense with a future time marker expresses a scheduled event. Same as English 'she's getting married next month'. Casarse = to get married (reflexive).

Hice un gesto de asentimiento. —Iré a la boda —murmuré al recordar que no podía verme. —Claro, lo sé. Los vestidos de las damas de honor son preciosos, ¿no crees?
I nodded, remembered she couldn't see me, and mumbled, "I'm in the wedding." "Oh, sure, I knew that. Pretty dresses for the bridesmaids, don't you think?"

A decir verdad, lo último en lo que me apetecía gastarme ciento veinte dólares era un vestido largo de color rosa con mangas de farol, pero era la boda de Catherine.
Actually, the last thing I wanted to spend a hundred and twenty dollars on was a long pink formal with puffy sleeves, but it was Catherine's wedding.
vocabulario

  • gastarme — Reflexive gastarse: to spend money on oneself. The reflexive adds a nuance of personal involvement/cost, common with spending verbs in Spanish.
19 💬 Anita / Mónica
diálogo
—¿Qué pasa con la fiesta? —Oh, me estoy yendo por las ramas, ¿verdad? Y tú muerta de sueño… Pensé en pegarle un grito para ver si conseguía que abreviara. No: probablemente, se echaría a llorar.
"What about the bachelorette party?" "Oh, I'm rambling, aren't I? And you just desperate for sleep." I wondered if screaming at her would make her go away any faster. Naw, she'd probably cry.
vocabulario

gramática
  • irse por las ramas — Fixed idiom: to digress, to waffle. The metaphor is of a bird leaving the trunk to go into the branches — straying from the main point.
  • muerta de sueño — Muerto/a de + noun: idiomatic intensifier for exhaustion, hunger, boredom, etc. Muerto de hambre (starving), muerto de risa (dying of laughter). Gendered to agree with the subject.
  • conseguir que + subjuntivo — Conseguir (to manage to / succeed in) takes subjunctive in the subordinate clause when subjects differ. Conseguía que abreviara = managed to get her to be brief.
  • echarse a + infinitivo — Inchoative construction: to suddenly start doing something. Echarse a llorar = to burst into tears. Other common ones: echarse a reír, echarse a correr.

21 💬 Mónica / Anita (narración)
diálogo
—La fiesta será esta noche. Como Catherine dice que no bebes, he pensado que podrías conducir. Me quedé recostada un momento, intentando decidir hasta qué punto cabrearme y si serviría de algo. Quizá, si hubiera estado más despierta, no habría dicho lo que pensaba.
"The bachelorette party is tonight. Catherine says you don't drink, so I was wondering if you could be designated driver." I just lay there for a minute, wondering how mad to get, and if it would do me any good. Maybe if I'd been more awake, I wouldn't have said what I was thinking.
vocabulario

gramática
  • como Catherine dice que no bebes — Como as causal conjunction (= ya que / puesto que): 'since / given that'. This causal como typically appears at the start of a clause and takes indicative.
  • he pensado que podrías — Softened request: pensar que + condicional. The conditional (podrías) distances the request, making it more polite or tentative. Equivalent to 'I was thinking maybe you could…'
  • si hubiera estado… no habría dicho — Período hipotético del pasado (tipo 3): si + pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo, condicional compuesto. Unreal condition in the past. 'If she had been more awake, she wouldn't have said it' — but she wasn't, and she did.
22 💬 Anita / Mónica
diálogo
—¿No te parece que, si querías que condujera, tendrías que haberme avisado un poco antes? —Lo sé, y te pido mil disculpas. Últimamente ando muy despistada.
"Don't you think this is awfully short notice, since you want me to drive?" "I know. I'm so sorry. I'm just so scattered lately."
vocabulario
¿no te parece que?don't you think that…?

gramática
  • si querías que condujera — Querer que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: when the main verb is in the past, the subordinate takes imperfect subjunctive. Conducir → condujera (irregular -ra subjunctive from the preterite stem conduj-).
  • tendrías que haber avisado — Tener que + infinitivo compuesto en condicional: obligation in the past that wasn't fulfilled. 'You should have warned me.' Tener que + haber + participio = should have done.
  • andar + adjetivo — Andar as a linking verb expressing an ongoing state or behaviour: ando muy liada (I've been really busy). More dynamic than estar; implies movement through a state over time.
23 💬 Mónica / Anita (narración)
Catherine dice que sueles librar el viernes o el sábado por la noche. ¿No tendrás libre el viernes de esta semana? Pues sí, aquel día libraba, aunque no me apetecía nada dedicárselo a la cabeza hueca con la que estaba hablando.
"Catherine told me you usually have either Friday or Saturday night off. Is Friday not your night off this week?" As a matter of fact it was, but I didn't really want to give up my only night off to this airhead on the other end of the phone.
vocabulario
sueles libraryou usually have off (soler + inf. = usually do)
librarto have a day off (work)
¿no tendrás libre?you wouldn't happen to have off, would you? (future of supposition)
la cabeza huecaairhead, empty-headed person
dedicárseloto give it over to / devote it to
no me apetecía nadaI really didn't feel like it at all
gramática
  • sueles librar — Soler + infinitivo: habitual action. Soler has no future or conditional; for those tenses, use acostumbrar or a circumlocution. Suelo levantarme temprano = I usually get up early.
  • ¿no tendrás libre? — Futuro de probabilidad in a question: tentative inquiry. Monica is hedging — 'I wonder if you might happen to have…' Less direct than ¿tienes libre?
  • la cabeza hueca — Idiomatic noun phrase (lit. 'hollow head'). Functions as a noun: es una cabeza hueca = she's an airhead. The relative clause con la que estaba hablando anchors it to Monica without naming her again.

—Sí; tengo la noche libre. —¡Estupendo! Te daré la dirección; puedes recogernos después del trabajo. ¿Te viene bien? —Vale. —No me iba bien, pero ¿qué iba a decir?
"I do have the night off." "Great! I'll give you directions, and you can pick us up after work. Is that okay?" It wasn't, but what else could I say. "That's fine."
vocabulario
tengo la noche libreI have the night off
estupendogreat, wonderful

valeokay, fine (Spain)
¿qué iba a decir?what was I going to say? / what could I say?
gramática
  • ¿te viene bien? — Venir bien: to suit, to be convenient, to work out. Like gustar, the thing is the subject: el viernes me viene bien = Friday works for me. Very common in Spain for scheduling.
  • ¿qué iba a decir? — Ir a + infinitivo en imperfecto as a rhetorical question expressing inevitability or lack of options. 'What was I supposed to say?' The imperfect of ir a suggests the action was never really going to happen differently.

"Pencil and paper?" "You said you worked with Catherine, right?" I was actually beginning to remember Monica. "Why, yes." "I know where Catherine works. I don't need directions."
vocabulario
¿tienes para apuntar?do you have something to write with? (lit. do you have [something] to jot down?)

empezando a acordarmebeginning to remember (acordarse de = to remember)

gramática
  • ¿tienes para apuntar? — Elliptical construction: tener para + infinitivo = to have [something] with which to do something. The object is implied (paper, pen). Common colloquial shorthand.
  • empezando a acordarme — Empezar a + infinitivo: inchoative — beginning to do. Acordarse de (to remember) is reflexive; here in gerundio: empezando a acordarme = starting to remember.
  • no me hace falta — Hacer falta = to be necessary/needed. With dative: me hace falta = I need it. More impersonal than necesito; the thing that is needed is grammatically the subject.

"Oh, how silly of me, of course. Then we'll see you about five. Dress up, but no heels. We may be dancing tonight." I hate to dance. "Sure, see you then." "See you tonight."
vocabulario

gramática
  • puede que + subjuntivo — Puede que always takes subjunctive. Expresses possibility: 'it may be that / maybe'. Compare: quizás vayamos, tal vez vayamos — all subjunctive for uncertainty.

  • no te pongas tacones — Ponerse + prenda: to put on / to wear. Negative imperative with reflexive: no te pongas (don't put on / don't wear). Poner → pongas in subjunctive.

Mónica era compañera de trabajo de Catherine, y eso significaba que era abogada. Era una idea inquietante. Quizá fuera una de esas personas que sólo son organizadas en el trabajo. No, ni de coña.
Monica worked with Catherine, that made her a lawyer. That was a frightening thought. Maybe she was one of those people who was only organized at work. Naw.
vocabulario
compañera de trabajowork colleague, coworker

gramática
  • quizá fuera — Quizá/quizás + subjuntivo o indicativo: when uncertainty is high, subjunctive is used. Quizá fuera (imperfect subj.) is more doubtful than quizá era (indicative, slightly more confident). Both are correct; the choice signals degree of doubt.
  • ni de coña — Very colloquial negation (Castilian). Coña is vulgar slang; the full phrase means 'not a chance / absolutely not'. Variants: ni hablar, ni en sueños, de ninguna manera (progressively more formal).

Entonces, cuando ya era demasiado tarde, se me ocurrió que podía haber rechazado la invitación. Arg. Pues sí que andaba bien de reflejos.
It occurred to me then, when it was too late, that I could just have refused the invitation. Damn. I was quick today.
vocabulario
se me ocurrióit occurred to me (pretérito)
podía haber rechazadoI could have refused (conditional sense in past)
rechazarto refuse, to reject
la invitaciónthe invitation
Arg.Ugh. / Damn. (interjection of frustration)
andar bien de reflejosto have great reflexes (ironic)
gramática
  • se me ocurrió — Ocurrírsele a alguien: impersonal construction, like gustar. The idea is the grammatical subject; the person is dative (me). 'An idea occurred to me' = literally 'an idea occurred itself to me'.
  • podía haber rechazado — Imperfecto de poder + infinitivo compuesto: past ability to have done something (but didn't). Functions like a conditional perfect in context: 'I could have refused.' Compare the conditional: podría haber rechazado (more hypothetical).
  • Pues sí que andaba bien de reflejos. — Heavy irony. Andar bien de algo = to be well-supplied with something. She's mocking her own slow thinking. Pues sí que = 'well, sure / oh yes indeed' — sarcastic affirmation.

Bueno, tampoco iba a ser tan terrible ver a unas desconocidas ponerse como una cuba. Vamos, que con un poco de suerte, alguna me vomitaría en el coche.
Oh, well, how bad could it be? Watching strangers get blitzed out of their minds. If I was lucky, maybe someone would throw up in my car.
vocabulario
tampoco iba a ser tan terribleit wasn't going to be that terrible either

vamos, queI mean / basically / the thing is (colloquial connector)

gramática
  • tampoco iba a ser — Ir a + infinitivo en imperfecto for a near-future in the past, with tampoco (neither / not… either) adding resignation. 'It wasn't going to be that bad either' — comparing implicitly to her other miseries.
  • ponerse como una cuba — Fixed idiom. Una cuba = a barrel (for wine/spirits). Getting drunk = becoming full like a barrel. Related: estar como una cuba (to be drunk), ponerse como un tomate (to turn red/to blush).
  • vamos, que — Discourse marker introducing a summary or clarification. Colloquial, very Spanish. 'Basically / I mean / the point is'. Often signals a slightly exasperated or resigned conclusion.

vocabulario

  • conseguí volver a dormirme — Three verbs chained: conseguir (managed) + volver a (again) + dormirme (to fall asleep). Volver a + infinitivo is the standard way to say 'to do something again' (preferred over dormirme de nuevo/otra vez in literary style).
  • una mujer a la que no conocía — Relativo con preposición: a la que (whom — with personal a). When a relative pronoun is the object of a verb that takes a (conocer a alguien), the a carries into the relative clause. Could also be a quien or informally just que.
