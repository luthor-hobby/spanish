Placeres Prohibidos

Laurell K. Hamilton · Capítulo 3: Tres

Lectura glosada para el estudio del español — cada oración con glosa, vocabulario y notas gramaticales

Contexto del capítulo

Anita, Catherine y Mónica salen a la despedida de soltera. Mónica lleva una chapa que ya avanza adónde se dirigen: el Placeres Prohibidos, el único club de boys vampíricos del Distrito. En la puerta conocemos al vampiro portero Buzz y, dentro, al dueño del local: Jean-Claude, maestro vampiro de dos siglos de antigüedad que tiene una fascinación especial por Anita. Ella le entrega su crucifijo, queda desarmada, y comienza el espectáculo.

§ Las tres mujeres
01
Mónica Vespucci llevaba una chapa con la inscripción LOS VAMPIROS TAMBIÉN SON PERSONAS. No era un comienzo muy prometedor para la velada.
Monica Vespucci was wearing a button that said, "Vampires are People, too." It was not a promising beginning to the evening.
vocabulario
llevaba una chapawas wearing a badge/button (chapa = pin badge)
la inscripciónthe inscription, the text on it
prometedorpromising
la veladathe evening (social event)
gramática
  • llevaba — Imperfecto for a state or appearance at a moment in the past. Llevar + clothing/accessory = to be wearing. More vivid than tenía puesta (had on). The imperfect sets the scene before the narrative action begins.
  • No era un comienzo muy prometedor — Imperfect again for a background evaluation. Ser in imperfect describes a characteristic or quality, not a punctual event. Anita's dry assessment hangs over the whole chapter.
02
Llevaba una blusa blanca de seda, con cuello alto y volantes que le resaltaba el bronceado de salón de belleza. Tenía el pelo corto y con peinado de estilista, y su maquillaje era perfecto. La chapa debería haberme dado una pista sobre el tipo de despedida de soltera que había organizado. Pero hay días en los que, simplemente, estoy lela.
Her white blouse was silk with a high, flared collar framing a dark, health-club tan. Her hair was short and expertly cut; her makeup, perfect. The button should have tipped me off to what kind of bachelorette party she'd planned. Some days I'm just slow to catch on.
vocabulario
volantesruffles, flounces (on clothing)
resaltarto highlight, to set off, to enhance
el bronceado de salón de bellezasalon/health-club tan
peinado de estilistaprofessionally styled hairdo
debería haberme dado una pistashould have tipped me off / given me a clue
estoy lelaI'm slow / I'm out of it / I'm clueless (colloquial)
gramática
  • que le resaltaba el bronceado — Relative clause: que refers to cuello alto y volantes. Resaltar works like gustar: the collar is the subject, and le is the dative (to her). 'The collar which highlighted her tan for her.'
  • debería haberme dado — Deber en condicional + infinitivo compuesto: past moral obligation that was not fulfilled. 'Should have given me a clue' (but didn't). Same structure as tendría que haber + participio.
  • estoy lela — Lelo/a: colloquial for dull-witted, slow, dazed. Estar (not ser) because it describes a temporary state. Related: estar en las nubes (to have one's head in the clouds).
03
Yo llevaba vaqueros negros, botas de caña alta y una blusa granate. Me había arreglado el pelo para que combinara con el atuendo; los rizos negros me caían justo por encima de los hombros. El color marrón oscuro, casi negro, de los ojos me hace juego con el pelo. La piel, demasiado pálida, germánica, contrasta con el moreno latino de todo lo demás.
I was wearing black jeans, knee-high boots, and a crimson blouse. My hair was made to order for the outfit, black curling just over the shoulders of the red blouse. The solid, nearly black-brown of my eyes matches the hair. Only the skin stands out, too pale, Germanic against the Latin darkness.
vocabulario

botas de caña altaknee-high boots (caña = shaft of a boot)

gramática
  • me había arreglado el pelo para que combinara — Para que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: purpose clause with different subjects. She arranged her hair (she did it) so that it would match (the hair matches). Combinar → combinara (imperfect subjunctive).
  • me caían justo por encima de los hombros — Caer for how hair or fabric falls/hangs. The reflexive dative me indicates on/over her. Justo = exactly/just.
  • me hace juego con el pelo — Hacer juego con: fixed expression for matching/complementing. Like gustar, juego is the subject: the eye colour is what 'makes a match' with her hair.
04
Un ex, muy ex, me describió en una ocasión como una muñequita de porcelana. Creo que lo dijo como un cumplido, pero yo no lo interpreté así. Es uno de los muchos motivos por los que no salgo a menudo con hombres.
A very ex-boyfriend once described me as a little china doll. He meant it as a compliment. I didn't take it that way. There are reasons why I don't date much.
vocabulario
un ex, muy exa very ex-boyfriend / a long-gone ex
una muñequita de porcelanaa little china doll
un cumplidoa compliment
interpretarto interpret, to take (a remark)
salir conto date, to go out with
gramática
  • un ex, muy ex — Emphatic repetition with muy modifying the noun used as adjective. Colloquial intensification: not just an ex, but a very-much ex. The comma signals the pause/stress.
  • lo dijo como un cumplido / lo interpreté así — Lo as neuter pronoun referring to the whole previous idea (describing her as a doll). Así = that way. The neuter pronoun absorbs the entire preceding clause.
  • Es uno de los muchos motivos por los que — Por los que: relative pronoun after por. Anita reduces the anecdote to an entry in a running list. The understatement is the joke.
05
Me había puesto una blusa de manga larga, para ocultar la funda del cuchillo que llevaba en la muñeca derecha y las cicatrices que tengo en el brazo izquierdo. Había dejado la pistola en el maletero. No creía que la despedida fuera a desmadrarse tanto.
The blouse was long-sleeved to hide the knife sheath on my right wrist and the scars on my left arm. I had left my gun locked in the trunk of my car. I didn't think the bachelorette party would get that out of hand.
vocabulario
manga largalong sleeve
la funda del cuchillothe knife sheath/holster
la muñecathe wrist; also: doll
las cicatricesthe scars
el maleterothe (car) trunk/boot
desmadrarseto get out of hand, to go wild (desmadre = chaos)
gramática
  • para ocultar — Para + infinitivo: purpose. The long sleeves exist in order to hide. Notice she chose the blouse deliberately; the purpose is hers.
  • No creía que… fuera a desmadrarse — No creer que + subjuntivo: negated belief triggers subjunctive. Ir a + infinitivo in imperfect subjunctive: fuera a desmadrarse = was going to get out of hand. A double structure: subjunctive mood + periphrastic future inside it.
§ Calle abajo
06 💬 Mónica / Anita
diálogo
—Siento mucho haberlo organizado todo en el último momento, Catherine —dijo Mónica—. Por eso somos sólo tres. Las demás habían hecho planes. —Qué curioso que la gente tenga planes un viernes por la noche —dije.
"I'm so sorry that I put off planning this to the last minute, Catherine. That's why there's only three of us. Everybody else had plans," Monica said. "Imagine that, people having plans for Friday night," I said.
vocabulario
siento mucho haberlo organizado en el último momentoI'm so sorry I left the planning to the last minute
las demásthe others, the rest (of the women)
habían hecho planeshad made plans (pluperfect)
qué curioso quehow strange that / imagine that (ironic)
gramática
  • siento mucho haberlo organizado — Sentir + infinitivo compuesto: regretting a past action. Subject is the same (Monica) so infinitive is used, not subjunctive. Compare: siento que lo hayas organizado así (different subject → subjunctive).
  • qué curioso que la gente tenga — Qué + adjective + que + subjuntivo: exclamatory with subjunctive. Anita's sarcasm is bone dry. The subjunctive here expresses the emotional reaction to a fact.
07
Mónica se quedó mirándome sin saber si bromeaba o no. Catherine me lanzó una mirada de advertencia. Les dediqué a ambas mi mejor sonrisa inocente. Mónica me la devolvió; Catherine no se dejó enredar.
Monica stared at me as if trying to decide whether I was joking or not. Catherine gave me a warning glare. I gave them both my best angelic smile. Monica smiled back. Catherine wasn't fooled.
vocabulario
se quedó mirándomeshe stood there staring at me
sin saber si bromeabanot knowing whether I was joking
una mirada de advertenciaa warning look/glare
les dediqué a ambasI gave to both of them
me la devolvióshe returned it to me (the smile)
no se dejó enredarshe wasn't fooled / didn't take the bait
gramática
  • se quedó mirándome — Quedarse + gerundio: to remain doing something. More vivid than me miró; implies she stayed in that posture, frozen, staring.
  • les dediqué a ambas mi mejor sonrisa — Double dative for emphasis: les (indirect object pronoun) + a ambas (explicit noun). Spanish often doubles the dative this way for clarity or stress. Dedicar here = to direct, to bestow.
  • no se dejó enredar — Dejarse + infinitivo: to allow oneself to be done to. Enredar = to entangle, to trick. No se dejó enredar = she didn't let herself be taken in. Very idiomatic; compare dejarse llevar (to go with the flow).
08
Mónica echó a andar calle abajo, alegre como unas castañuelas borrachas. Y sólo se había tomado dos copas durante la cena. Mala señal.
Monica began dancing down the sidewalk, happy as a drunken clam. She had had only two drinks with dinner. It was a bad sign.
vocabulario
echó a andarset off walking, started walking
calle abajodown the street
alegre como unas castañuelas borrachashappy as a drunken clam (lit. drunk castanets)
las castañuelascastanets (percussion instrument)
se había tomado dos copasshe had had two drinks
mala señalbad sign
gramática
  • echó a andar — Echar a + infinitivo: inchoative — to suddenly start doing. Echar a andar/correr/reír/llorar are the most common. Abrupt, dynamic beginning of an action.
  • alegre como unas castañuelas borrachas — Spanish simile (the English equivalent is 'happy as a clam'). Castanets are associated with festive, rhythmic joy; borrachas (drunk) is the translator's humorous addition. The simile structure: adj. + como + noun phrase.
  • Mala señal. — Nominal sentence (no verb). The fragment pattern — subject/noun + no verb — recurs throughout for Anita's terse, deadpan interior commentary.
09 💬 Catherine / Anita
diálogo
—Sé amable —susurró Catherine. —¿Y ahora qué he hecho? —Anita… —me dijo con una voz que sonaba como la de mi padre cuando yo volvía a casa demasiado tarde.
"Be nice," Catherine whispered. "What did I say?" "Anita." Her voice sounded like my father's used to sound when I'd stayed out too late.
vocabulario
sé amablebe nice (imperative of ser)
susurrówhispered (pretérito of susurrar)
¿y ahora qué he hecho?now what have I done? (exasperated)
sonaba como la de mi padresounded like my father's
cuando yo volvía a casa demasiado tardewhen I used to come home too late
gramática
  • sé amable — Irregular imperative of ser (tú): sé. Not to be confused with sé from saber (I know). Context distinguishes them.
  • ¿y ahora qué he hecho? — Rhetorical. Y ahora = 'now what'. Pasado compuesto (he hecho) for an action with present relevance: 'what have I (just) done?' The y at the start adds exasperation.
  • cuando yo volvía a casa demasiado tarde — Imperfecto for a habitual past action: 'when I used to come home late'. The imperfect captures the repeated pattern, not a single occasion.
10 💬 Anita / Catherine
diálogo
—No se te ve muy animada esta noche —dije con un suspiro. —Pues tengo la intención de animarme mucho —repuso alzando los brazos. Iba todavía con el traje de oficina, lleno de arrugas. El viento le agitaba el pelo largo y cobrizo.
"You're just no fun tonight," I sighed. "I plan to be a lot of fun tonight." She stretched her arms skyward. She still wore the crumpled remains of her business suit. The wind blew her long, copper-colored hair.
vocabulario
no se te ve muy animadayou don't look very lively / you seem a bit flat
con un suspirowith a sigh
tengo la intención deI intend to, I plan to
animarmeto have fun, to liven up (reflexive)

gramática
  • no se te ve muy animada — Impersonal passive with dative of interest: se ve (one can see / it shows) + te (dative, for you/on you). 'You don't look very animated tonight.' The se here is passivising, not reflexive.
  • repuso — Literary synonym for dijo meaning to reply/retort. Preterite of reponer. Common in narrative prose; rarely used in everyday speech.
  • alzando los brazos — Gerundio as simultaneous action: she raised her arms as she spoke. Equivalent to the English participial phrase 'stretching her arms skyward'.
11
Nunca he sabido si Catherine estaría más guapa si se cortara el pelo para que se le viera bien la cara, o si es el pelo lo que la hace tan atractiva.
I've never been able to decide if Catherine would be prettier if she cut her hair, so you'd notice the face first, or if the hair was what made her pretty.
vocabulario
nunca he sabidoI've never known / been able to decide
estaría más guapawould be prettier (conditional)
se cortara el peloshe cut her hair (imperfect subjunctive)
para que se le viera bien la caraso that her face would be seen clearly
lo que la hace tan atractivawhat makes her so attractive
gramática
  • si se cortara… estaría — Período hipotético tipo 2: si + imperfecto de subjuntivo, condicional simple. Open hypothetical: if she were to cut her hair, she would be prettier. The situation is possible but not actual.
  • para que se le viera bien la cara — Para que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: purpose. Se le viera = her face would be seen (passive se + dative le). This triple structure — purpose + passive se + dative — is dense but idiomatic.
  • es el pelo lo que la hace tan atractiva — Construcción enfática con lo que: cleft sentence. 'It is the hair that makes her so attractive.' The lo que focuses attention on el pelo as the key element.
12 💬 Catherine / Anita
diálogo
—Si tengo que renunciar a una de mis pocas noches libres —añadió—, tengo intención de divertirme… un huevo. —Pronunció las dos últimas palabras como con rabia. Me quedé mirándola. —No pensarás ponerte ciega de alcohol, ¿verdad? —Tal vez —dijo. Parecía muy ufana.
"If I have to give up one of my few free nights, then I am going to enjoy myself—immensely," she said. There was a kind of fierceness to the last word. I stared up at her. "You are not planning to get falling-down drunk, are you?" "Maybe." She looked smug.
vocabulario
renunciar ato give up, to forgo
un huevoa lot / immensely (vulgar intensifier, lit. 'an egg')
como con rabiawith a kind of fierceness / as if angrily
ponerse ciega de alcoholto get blind drunk
ufanasmug, self-satisfied, pleased with herself
gramática
  • si tengo que renunciar… tengo intención de divertirme — Si + indicativo, indicativo: real condition (not hypothetical — she does have to give up her night). Contrast with the subjunctive hypotheticals earlier in the chapter.
  • un huevo — Vulgar Castilian intensifier equivalent to 'a lot / massively / immensely'. Functions as an adverb of quantity: me duele un huevo (it hurts like hell). Very informal; register-aware usage matters here.
  • no pensarás ponerte ciega — Pensar + infinitivo: to plan to, to intend to. In question form with future (pensarás): expresses surprised disbelief. Ponerse ciego/a de algo: to gorge/overindulge on something.
13
Catherine sabía que yo no aprobaba o, mejor dicho, no comprendía que la gente bebiera. A mí no me hacía gracia desinhibirme; si quería desmadrarme, quería controlar hasta qué punto.
Catherine knew I didn't approve of, or rather, didn't understand drinking. I didn't like having my inhibitions lowered. If I was going to cut loose, I wanted to be in control of just how loose I got.
vocabulario
o, mejor dichoor rather, or more precisely

no me hacía graciaI didn't like / it didn't appeal to me
desinhibirseto lose one's inhibitions

gramática
  • no aprobaba que la gente bebiera — Verbo de emoción/valoración + que + subjuntivo: aprobar here = to approve of. The subjunctive (bebiera) is required because the main verb expresses an attitude toward the subordinate action.
  • o, mejor dicho — Reformulation marker. Very common in speech and writing when the speaker corrects or refines what they just said. Equivalent to 'or rather / to be more precise'.
  • a mí no me hacía gracia — Hacer gracia: to appeal, to find funny/enjoyable. Like gustar: the thing is the subject, the person is dative. The a mí is emphatic (contrastive with others who might enjoy it).
§ El Distrito
14
Habíamos dejado el coche a dos manzanas, en un aparcamiento. El que tiene alrededor una valla de hierro forjado. No había muchos sitios para aparcar cerca del río; las estrechas calles adoquinadas y las aceras anticuadas del casco antiguo estaban pensadas para caballos, no para automóviles.
We had left my car in a parking lot two blocks back. The one with the wrought-iron fence around it. There wasn't much parking down by the river. The narrow brick roads and ancient sidewalks had been designed for horses, not automobiles.
vocabulario
a dos manzanastwo blocks away
un aparcamientoa car park, parking lot
una valla de hierro forjadoa wrought-iron fence

gramática
  • El que tiene alrededor una valla — Relative clause as a standalone sentence fragment: 'The one with the fence around it.' Spanish allows this ellipsis; the antecedent (el aparcamiento) is clear from context. Gives a staccato, descriptive rhythm.
  • estaban pensadas para — Estar + participio: resultant state. Pensadas (designed, intended) agrees with calles y aceras (fem. pl.). The streets don't just happen to accommodate horses — they were deliberately built for them.
15
Una tormenta de verano que había empezado y terminado mientras cenábamos había refrescado las calles. Las primeras estrellas brillaban por encima de nosotras como diamantes cosidos a un paño de terciopelo.
The streets had been fresh-washed by a summer thunderstorm that had come and gone while we ate dinner. The first stars glittered overhead, like diamonds trapped in velvet.
vocabulario

gramática
  • que había empezado y terminado mientras cenábamos — Pluperfect in a relative clause. The storm started and ended (pluperfect — completed before the reference moment) while they were eating (imperfect — ongoing action). Three time layers in one clause: narration time, dinner time, storm time.
  • como diamantes cosidos a un paño de terciopelo — Extended simile. Cosidos (sewn) is a past participle used as an adjective, modifying diamantes. The image of stars as sewn-on diamonds elevates Anita's narration into the literary register — a rare moment of beauty before she turns to pragmatics.

diálogo

17 💬 Catherine / Anita
diálogo
Quizá, si hubiera bebido en la cena, yo también habría echado a correr, aunque tenía serias dudas. —No seas quejica —me gritó Catherine. ¿Quejica? Les di alcance caminando.
Maybe if I'd had drinks with dinner, I'd have run, too, but I doubted it. "Don't be an old stick in the mud," Catherine called back. Stick in the mud? I caught up to them walking.
vocabulario

gramática
  • si hubiera bebido… habría echado — Período hipotético tipo 3: past unreal condition. She didn't drink, so she didn't run. The pluperfect subjunctive + conditional perfect express what would have happened in an unrealised past scenario.
  • ¿Quejica? — Rhetorical echo question. Anita repeats the word in disbelief. No verb, pure ironic deflection. A classic Hamilton fragment.
  • les di alcance caminando — Dar alcance: to catch up to. Caminando is a gerundio of manner — she caught up by walking (not running), underlining her refusal to join in.
18 💬 Mónica / Anita (narración)
diálogo
Mónica se calmó lo suficiente para fingir un susurro teatral. —¿Sabéis qué hay al doblar la esquina? La verdad era que lo sabía. El último asesinato de un vampiro había sucedido a sólo cuatro manzanas de allí. Estábamos en la zona que los vampiros llamaban el Distrito.
Monica calmed enough to fake an ominous stage whisper. "Do you know what lies around this corner?" As a matter of fact, I did. The last vampire killing had been only four blocks from here. We were in what the vampires called "the District."
vocabulario

  • lo suficiente para fingir — Lo suficiente para + infinitivo: enough to do something. Lo is a neuter article nominalising suficiente. Compare: habló lo suficiente para convencerme.

  • La verdad era que lo sabía — La verdad era que: emphatic concessive opener. 'The truth of the matter was that I did know.' Signals Anita stepping back from the shared scene to share privileged information with the reader.
19 💬 Anita / Mónica / Catherine
diálogo

§ La entrada
20
El letrero era una espiral de neón rojo sangre. No se me escapó el simbolismo. Subimos los tres amplios escalones y vimos a un vampiro delante de la puerta abierta.
The sign was a wonderful swirling neon the color of heart blood. The symbolism was not lost on me. We went up three broad steps, and there was a vampire standing in front of the propped-open door.
vocabulario

gramática
  • no se me escapó el simbolismo — Escapársele algo a alguien: for something to escape/elude someone. Like gustar: the symbolism is the subject, Anita is the experiencer (me). 'The symbolism didn't escape me' = she caught it fully.
  • vimos a un vampiro — Personal a with an indefinite animate object. Spanish uses the personal a before direct objects that are people (or personalised beings like vampires), even indefinite ones. 'We saw a vampire' → vimos a un vampiro, not *vimos un vampiro.

gramática
  • no tuve más remedio que sonreír — No tener más remedio que + infinitivo: to have no choice but to. Fixed expression. Remedio here = remedy/recourse, not medicine. 'There was nothing for it but to smile.'
  • estás de guasa — Estar de guasa: to be joking, to be taking the mickey (Spain, colloquial). Guasa = joke, banter. Related: de broma, en serio (seriously).
  • por mis niños — Colloquial oath formula equivalent to 'scout's honour / cross my heart'. Swearing by one's children is a strong informal guarantee in Spanish culture. The English 'scout's honor' is a free equivalent; the Spanish is more personal.

gramática
  • como si no estuviera allí — Como si + imperfecto de subjuntivo: as if he weren't there. The como si construction always takes the imperfect (or pluperfect) subjunctive. The imperfect of estar is estuviera/estuviese.
  • me lanzó un beso — Lanzar un beso: to blow a kiss (lit. to throw/launch a kiss). Dative me = at me / to me. The predatory playfulness of the gesture closes the chapter perfectly.
  • empezaba el espectáculo — Imperfect for a beginning action, or as a literary present-becoming-past. The inverted order (verb before subject) gives it a theatrical, curtain-raising feel. Chapter-closing sentence with maximum atmosphere.
