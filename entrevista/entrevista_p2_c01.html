Entrevista con el Vampiro

Anne Rice · Capítulo 1: Segunda parte

Pasaje 1 de 8

01
Durante toda la noche estuve en la cubierta del barco francés Mariana observando a los estibadores —prosiguió el vampiro—. El muelle estaba lleno de gente y las fiestas duraron hasta tarde en las cabinas de lujo; las cubiertas estaban ahítas de pasajeros e invitados.
All night I stood on the deck of the French ship Mariana, watching the stevedores—the vampire continued. The dock was full of people, and the parties went on late into the night in the first-class cabins; the decks were glutted with passengers and guests.

ahítas deglutted/gorged with — normally used of people overfull from eating; here applied to the decks packed with people, making the crowd feel excessive, almost nauseating

02
Pero, por último, a medida que se aproximaba la madrugada, las fiestas terminaron una tras otra y los carruajes abandonaron las calles del puerto. Unos pocos pasajeros retrasados subieron a bordo; una pareja se detuvo largo rato en la cercana pasarela. Pero Lestat y su aprendiz, si sobrevivieron al fuego (y yo estaba convencido de que así había sido), no llegaron al barco.
But at last, as dawn approached, the parties ended one after another and the carriages left the port streets. A few late passengers came aboard; a couple lingered a long while on the nearby gangway. But Lestat and his apprentice, if they had survived the fire—and I was convinced they had—never reached the ship.
vocabulario
a medida queas, in proportion as — marks a gradual or simultaneous process: a medida que pasa el tiempo = as time goes by
una tras otraone after another — fixed collocation; also uno tras otro
largo ratoa long while — rato = a span of time; largo rato is a very common collocation meaning quite some time
subir a bordoto come/go aboard — a bordo is a fixed phrase: estar a bordo = to be on board
gramática
  • si sobrevivieron al fuego (y yo estaba convencido de que así había sido) — Condicional de pasado con indicativo: the si-clause uses the indicative (not subjunctive) because Louis is speculating about a real past event, not a hypothetical. The parenthetical así había sido refers back to sobrevivieron.
03
subjuntivo
El equipaje había salido de nuestra casa por la tarde; y si había quedado algo que les pudiera revelar dónde estábamos, yo estaba seguro de que el incendio lo había destruido. No obstante, me quedé vigilante. Claudia se había encerrado en su cabina, con los ojos fijos en la cerradura. Pero Lestat no vino.
The luggage had left our house in the afternoon; and if anything remained that could reveal where we were, I was certain the fire had destroyed it. Even so, I kept watch. Claudia had locked herself in her cabin, her eyes fixed on the lock. But Lestat did not come.
vocabulario
no obstanteeven so, nevertheless — formal connective, stronger than pero; appears frequently in literary prose
quedarse vigilanteto keep watch, remain on guard — quedarse + adjective expresses a resulting state
con los ojos fijos enwith eyes fixed on — fixed collocation: fijos en always follows ojos in this construction
encerrarse ento shut/lock oneself in — encerrar = to lock in; reflexive encerrarse = to lock oneself away
gramática
  • que les pudiera revelar — Subjuntivo en cláusula relativa: the subjunctive pudiera (imperfect subj. of poder) is triggered because the antecedent (algo) is indefinite/hypothetical. Compare: algo que les podía revelar (indicative) would assert it definitely existed.
04
Por último, tal como yo esperaba, la conmoción de zarpar dio comienzo antes del alba. Unas pocas personas saludaban desde el puerto y el espacio grasiento del muelle mientras el barco empezó, primero, a temblar, luego a sacudirse violentamente a un costado y luego a deslizarse con movimiento majestuoso en la corriente del Mississippi.
At last, just as I had expected, the commotion of departure began before dawn. A few people waved from the port and the greasy expanse of the dock as the ship began, first, to shudder, then to lurch violently to one side, and then to glide with a majestic motion into the current of the Mississippi.
vocabulario
dar comienzoto begin, get underway — formal alternative to empezar; common in narrating events: dio comienzo la ceremonia
zarparto set sail, to depart (by ship) — specifically nautical; not used for other departures
a un costadoto one side — costado = side (of a body or ship); a un costado = sideways, to one side
gramática
  • empezó a temblar, luego a sacudirse... luego a deslizarse — Perífrasis verbal (empezar a + infinitivo) repeated in a tricolon. The repeated a links each infinitive to the same governing verb, building rhythm.
05
Las luces de Nueva Orleans se fueron apagando hasta que detrás de nosotros sólo hubo una fosforescencia pálida contra las nubes borrascosas. Estaba más exhausto que nunca; sin embargo, permanecí en la cubierta mientras pude ver esa luz, sintiendo que tal vez jamás la volvería a ver.
The lights of New Orleans slowly faded until behind us there was only a pale phosphorescence against the stormy clouds. I was more exhausted than I had ever been; yet I remained on deck as long as I could see that light, feeling that perhaps I would never see it again.
vocabulario
irse apagandoir + gerund = gradual progressive action — apagarse = to go out/fade; irse apagando = to fade away little by little. This construction (ir + gerund) emphasizes slow, gradual change
nubes borrascosasstormy clouds — borrasca = squall/storm; borrascoso = stormy, tempestuous
volver a verto see again — volver a + infinitive = to do something again: volvería a ver = would see again
gramática
06
En un momento, pasados los muelles de Freniere y de Pointe du Lac y, entonces, cuando pude ver el gran muro de chopos y cipreses alzándose verdes en la oscuridad cerca del agua, supe que ya era casi la mañana. Demasiado y peligrosamente cercana.
At a certain moment, past the docks of Freniere and Pointe du Lac, when I could see the great wall of poplars and cypresses rising green in the darkness near the water, I knew that morning was almost upon us. Too close, and dangerously so.
vocabulario
alzándose verdesrising green — the participle verdes is a predicative adjective agreeing with chopos y cipreses, describing how they appeared as they rose up
gran muro degreat wall of — muro used metaphorically for a solid mass of trees; common in landscape description
gramática
  • Pasados los muelles — Participio absoluto: a shortened absolute clause meaning 'once past / having passed the docks.' This construction omits una vez or cuando habíamos pasado.
07
subjuntivo
Y, cuando metí la llave en la cerradura de la cabina, sentí el mayor agotamiento que quizás haya sentido en toda mi vida. Jamás, en todos los años que había vivido con mi selecta familia, había conocido el miedo que experimenté esa noche, la vulnerabilidad, el terror puro. Y no iba a haber un súbito alivio. Ninguna súbita sensación de seguridad.
And when I turned the key in the cabin lock, I felt the greatest exhaustion I had perhaps ever known in all my life. Never, in all the years I had lived with my chosen family, had I known the fear I experienced that night, the vulnerability, the pure terror. And there was going to be no sudden relief. No sudden sense of safety.
vocabulario
meter la llave en la cerradurato put/turn the key in the lock — meter here means to insert; cerradura = lock (the mechanism, not the key)
selecta familiachosen family — selecta = select, chosen; Louis refers to himself, Claudia, and Lestat
súbitosudden — literary/formal synonym for repentino; common in Anne Rice's style
gramática
  • que quizás haya sentido — Subjuntivo con quizás: quizás/quizá + present subjunctive expresses uncertainty. If indicative were used (he sentido), it would assert the fact more confidently.
  • no iba a haber — Ir a + infinitivo en pasado: iba a haber = 'there was going to be' — the past future, expressing what Louis knew lay ahead.
08
subjuntivo
Únicamente ese alivio que al final impone el cansancio cuando ni el cuerpo ni la mente pueden soportar más el terror. Porque aunque ahora Lestat estuviera a muchos kilómetros de distancia de nosotros, él, con su resurrección, había despertado en mí una red de miedos complejos de los que no podía escapar.
Only that relief which exhaustion finally forces upon you when neither body nor mind can bear the terror any longer. Because though Lestat was now many kilometers from us, his resurrection had awakened in me a web of complex fears from which I could not escape.
vocabulario
imponer el cansanciowhat exhaustion forces upon you — imponer = to impose, force; the subject is el cansancio (exhaustion), which imposes relief by simply overwhelming the body
red de miedosweb of fears — red = net/web/network; despertar una red de = to awaken a tangle of
ni... ni...neither... nor... — double negative; requires no additional negation: ni el cuerpo ni la mente pueden (not no pueden)
gramática
  • aunque ahora Lestat estuviera — Subjuntivo concesivo: aunque + imperfect subjunctive = 'even though / even if' (presenting the concession as not fully asserted). With indicative (estaba) it would state the fact flatly.
09
Incluso cuando Claudia me dijo: ''Estamos a salvo, Louis, estamos a salvo'', y le susurré la palabra sí, pude recordar a Lestat en el marco de aquella puerta, y aquellos ojos bulbosos, aquella piel llena de cicatrices. ¿Cómo había regresado, cómo había triunfado sobre la muerte? ¿Cómo cualquier criatura podía sobrevivir a la ruina arrugada en que se había convertido?
Even when Claudia told me, "We are safe, Louis, we are safe," and I whispered the word yes, I could still see Lestat in the frame of that doorway, those bulging eyes, that skin covered in scars. How had he come back, how had he triumphed over death? How could any creature survive the shriveled ruin he had become?
vocabulario
estar a salvoto be safe — fixed expression; a salvo is an invariable phrase (not an adjective), so: estamos a salvo, not a salvos
en el marco de aquella puertain the frame of that doorway — marco de la puerta = doorframe; standing in the doorway
triunfar sobre la muerteto triumph over death — triunfar sobre is the standard collocation
ruina arrugadashriveled ruin — arrugado = wrinkled/shriveled; ruina used figuratively for what Lestat had become
gramática
10
subjuntivo
Fuera la respuesta que fuese, ¿qué significaba, no sólo para él sino para mí, para Claudia? Estábamos a salvo de él, pero... ¿estábamos a salvo de nosotros mismos?
Whatever the answer might be, what did it mean—not only for him, but for me, for Claudia? We were safe from him, but... were we safe from ourselves?
vocabulario
no sólo... sino...not only... but... — standard paired connective for adding elements
a salvo desafe from — a salvo de + noun = protected from, out of reach of
gramática
  • Fuera la respuesta que fuese — Subjuntivo de concesión libre: 'whatever the answer might be.' This doubled subjunctive pattern (fuera... que fuese) is a set construction for 'whatever X may be.' Both verbs are imperfect subjunctive of ser.
11
subjuntivo
Los pasajeros empezaron a ser víctimas de una extraña "fiebre". Sin embargo, el barco estaba sorprendentemente limpio, aunque, de tanto en tanto, se podían encontrar sus cuerpos, sin peso y resecos, como si hiciera días que estuvieran muertos. No obstante, seguía esa fiebre.
The passengers began to fall victim to a strange "fever." Yet the ship remained surprisingly clean, though from time to time their bodies could be found—weightless and desiccated, as if they had been dead for days. And yet the fever went on.
vocabulario
ser víctimas deto fall victim to — víctimas de is used collectively or with ser; caer víctima de is also common
de tanto en tantofrom time to time — synonym of de vez en cuando; slightly more literary
resecosdesiccated, bone-dry — reseco = parched, dried out; stronger than seco
gramática
  • como si hiciera días que estuvieran muertos — Como si + imperfecto de subjuntivo: 'as if it had been days since they were dead.' Como si always takes the subjunctive. Hacer here is the impersonal verb for elapsed time: hace días = it's been days.
12
Primero un pasajero sintió debilidad e hinchazón en la garganta; de vez en cuando había allí marcas y, otras veces, en otros sitios; a veces no había ninguna marca reconocible, aunque se abría una antigua herida y volvía a doler. Y, a veces, el pasajero, que dormía cada vez más a medida que avanzaba el viaje y que avanzaba la fiebre, se moría durmiendo.
First a passenger would feel weakness and swelling in the throat; from time to time there were marks there, and sometimes elsewhere; sometimes there were no recognizable marks at all, though an old wound would open and begin to ache again. And sometimes the passenger—sleeping more and more as the voyage advanced and the fever progressed—would die in his sleep.
vocabulario
de vez en cuandofrom time to time — one of the most common temporal expressions in Spanish; interchangeable with a veces but implies slightly more irregularity
cada vez másmore and more — cada vez más + adjective/adverb expresses progressive intensification; cada vez menos = less and less
a medida que avanzabaas it advanced/progressed — a medida que marks simultaneous gradual processes
morirse durmiendoto die in one's sleep — morirse (not just morir) is the colloquial/emotional form; durmiendo is a gerund functioning adverbially
gramática
13
Por tanto, hubo entierros en el mar en varias ocasiones mientras cruzábamos el Atlántico. Naturalmente temeroso de la fiebre, yo evitaba a los demás pasajeros, no deseaba estar con ellos en el salón de fumar, ni conocer sus historias ni oír sus sueños y esperanzas. Yo comía a solas.
Thus there were burials at sea on several occasions as we crossed the Atlantic. Naturally fearful of the fever, I avoided the other passengers; I had no wish to join them in the smoking lounge, or to learn their stories or hear their dreams and hopes. I ate alone.
vocabulario
entierros en el marburials at sea — entierro = burial (from enterrar = to bury in the ground); used at sea as a fixed expression even though the body is not buried in earth
salón de fumarsmoking lounge — salón = lounge/hall; de fumar marks its purpose
a solasalone, by oneself — fixed phrase: not solo used predicatively, but the invariable a solas which emphasizes solitude
gramática
14
Pero a Claudia le gustaba observar a los pasajeros, quedarse en cubierta y verlos ir y venir en el atardecer, para luego decirme en voz baja cuando me sentaba en las sillas de cubierta:
But Claudia liked to watch the passengers, to linger on deck and observe them coming and going at dusk, and then to murmur to me in a low voice when I sat down in the deck chairs:

quedarse en cubiertato stay/linger on deck — cubierta = ship's deck; quedarse emphasizes remaining in place
ir y venircoming and going — fixed idiom for back-and-forth movement

15 💬 Claudia
—Pienso que ella caerá víctima de... Yo bajaba después con mi libro y miraba por el ojo de buey, sintiendo la suave oscilación del mar, escrutando las estrellas, más claras y brillantes de lo que jamás eran en tierra, hundiéndose para tocar las olas.
"I think she will fall victim to..." Then I would go below with my book and gaze through the porthole, feeling the gentle roll of the sea, scanning the stars, clearer and brighter than they ever were on land, dipping down to touch the waves.
vocabulario
ojo de bueyporthole — literally 'ox's eye'; the standard Spanish term for a ship's circular window

16
Parecía, por momentos, cuando me sentaba a solas en la cabina a oscuras, que el cielo había bajado para encontrarse con las aguas y que en esa reunión se revelaría un gran secreto; algún gran golfo se cerraría milagrosamente para siempre. Pero, ¿quién iba a hacer semejante revelación cuando el cielo y el mar ya no se podían distinguir más y ya no era más que el caos? ¿Dios? ¿Satán?
It seemed, at moments, when I sat alone in the darkened cabin, that the sky had come down to meet the waters and that in that meeting a great secret would be revealed; some vast gulf would miraculously close forever. But who would make such a revelation when sky and sea could no longer be told apart and were nothing but chaos? God? Satan?
vocabulario
a oscurasin the dark — fixed phrase; oscuras is a plural form used only in this expression
por momentosat moments, at times — por momentos implies fleetingly or at intervals
no era más quewas nothing but — no... más que = only, nothing but; a dismissive reduction
gran golfovast gulf — golfo = gulf, chasm; used metaphorically for the gap between sky and sea, or between Louis and meaning
gramática
  • se revelaría... se cerraría — Condicional de especulación: Louis imagines what would happen in this cosmic meeting, so the conditional is appropriate for his speculation.
17
subjuntivo
De repente se me ocurrió qué consuelo sería conocer a Satán, mirarlo a la cara, por más terrible que fuera su aspecto, para saber que le pertenecía totalmente y, de ese modo, poner a descansar para siempre el tormento de esa ignorancia. Pasar a través de un velo que me separaba para siempre de todo lo que yo denominaba la naturaleza humana.
It suddenly occurred to me what comfort it would be to know Satan, to look him in the face, however terrible his aspect might be, to know that I belonged to him utterly, and in that way lay to rest forever the torment of that ignorance. To pass through a veil that separated me forever from all that I called human nature.
vocabulario
se me ocurrióit occurred to me — ocurrírsele a alguien = to occur to someone, to come to mind; the indirect object marks whose mind it came to
mirarlo a la carato look him in the face — mirar a alguien a la cara is the collocation; note the redundant lo and a la cara
poner a descansarto lay to rest — poner a descansar is a set phrase meaning to put something to rest permanently; used for worries, doubts, conflicts
pasar a través de un veloto pass through a veil — velo = veil; metaphor for the barrier between vampire and human nature
gramática
  • por más terrible que fuera su aspecto — Subjuntivo concesivo con por más que: 'however terrible his aspect might be.' Por más + adj + que + subjunctive is a fixed concessive pattern.
18
Sentí que el barco se aproximaba cada vez más a ese secreto. No había un final visible en el firmamento; se cerraba encima de nosotros con una belleza y un silencio sobrecogedores. Pero entonces las palabras poner a descansarse, hicieron horribles. Porque no habría descanso en la maldición, no podía haber descanso. ¿Y qué era este tormento comparado con los fuegos eternos del infierno?
I felt the ship drawing ever closer to that secret. There was no visible end to the firmament; it closed above us with an awe-inspiring beauty and silence. But then the words "lay to rest" turned horrible. Because there could be no rest in damnation, there could be no rest. And what was this torment compared to the eternal fires of hell?
vocabulario
cada vez másever closer, more and more — see earlier note; here modifies the verb phrase se aproximaba
sobrecogedorawe-inspiring, overwhelming — from sobrecoger = to overwhelm, seize; sobrecogedor describes something that strikes you breathless

19
El mar meciéndose bajo esas estrellas constantes —aquellas mismas estrellas—, ¿qué tenía que ver eso con Satán?
The sea rocking beneath those constant stars—those same stars—what did any of it have to do with Satan?

20
Y esas imágenes que nos parecen tan extáticas en nuestra infancia, cuando estamos todos convulsionados con el frenesí mortal que apenas nos podemos imaginar que son deseables; el serafín contemplando para siempre la faz de Dios— y la misma faz de Dios—, aquello era el descanso eterno, del cual este suave y mecedor océano sólo era una remota promesa.

21

22

23

24
—Tendríamos que haberlo enterrado —dijo—. Fuimos unos tontos en pensar que debido a su aspecto estaba muerto. —Pero, ¿cómo pudo haber sobrevivido? —le pregunté—. Tú lo viste, tú sabes en qué se convirtió. Yo, en realidad, no tenía ganas de ahondar en ello. Con todas mis ganas lo hubiera desterrado de mis pensamientos, pero mi mente no me lo permitió.
"We should have buried him," she said. "We were fools to think he was dead because of how he looked." "But how could he have survived?" I asked her. "You saw him, you know what he became." I had no real desire to dwell on it. With all my heart I would have banished him from my thoughts, but my mind would not allow it.
vocabulario
tendríamos que haberwe should have — tener que = to have to; in the conditional perfect, it expresses an unfulfilled obligation
ahondar ento dwell on, to go deeper into — ahondar = to deepen, probe; ahondar en un tema = to dig deeper into a subject
con todas mis ganaswith all my heart/desire — con ganas = eagerly, willingly; con todas mis ganas intensifies: with every fiber of my being

gramática
  • Tendríamos que haberlo enterrado — Condicional perfecto de obligación incumplida: 'we should have buried him.' Tener que + haber + past participle in the conditional = unfulfilled obligation.
  • Con todas mis ganas lo hubiera desterrado — Pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo (hipótesis irreal): 'I would have banished him.' The subjunctive pluperfect here replaces the conditional perfect in colloquial usage.
25

And it was she who then gave me the answers, for the dialogue was, in truth, with herself.

26 💬 Claudia / Louis
