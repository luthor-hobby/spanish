Entrevista con el Vampiro

Anne Rice · Capítulo 2: Primera parte

Pasaje 2

Primera parte — Pasaje 2

El vampiro construye un oratorio para su hermano y confía en él sus problemas cotidianos. El hermano empieza a tener visiones: la Virgen le ordena que venda las plantaciones. El vampiro se ríe de él. El hermano cae por la escalera y muere. El vampiro se siente culpable. Se hunde en el alcohol y la autodestrucción en Nueva Orleans, hasta que un vampiro lo ataca y casi lo desangra. Un sacerdote lo visita y atribuye todo al diablo — el vampiro, furioso, casi lo mata.

§ El oratorio — el hermano diferente
01 💬 Vampiro
subjuntivo
Por último, le construí un oratorio alejado de la casa y él empezó a pasar allí casi todo el día, y a menudo los atardeceres. Era algo irónico, en realidad. Él era tan diferente de nosotros, tan diferente de todos, ¡y yo era tan normal! No había nada extraordinario en mí. —El vampiro sonrió—.
In the end, I built him a chapel away from the house, and he began to spend almost all day there, and often the evenings too. It was rather ironic, really. He was so different from us, so different from everyone — and I was so ordinary! There was nothing extraordinary about me. The vampire smiled.
vocabulario
por últimoIn the end, lastly — signals the final step in a sequence of actions. Distinct from al final (in the end, as an outcome). Por último feels like 'and lastly' in a list.
alejado deAway from, set apart from — alejado de = removed from, at a distance from. Alejarse = to move away; alejado as adjective describes physical or emotional distance.
pasar el día/la tardePasar used for spending time: pasar el día (spend the day), pasar el rato (spend time, pass the time). Not to be confused with pasar = to pass/go by.
gramática
  • le construí un oratorio — Dative of interest: le marks the beneficiary (for him). The indirect object pronoun with construir indicates who the construction is for. Compare: le compré un libro (I bought him a book).
  • no había nada extraordinario en mí — En mí for 'about me' in existential statements. Spanish uses en where English uses 'about' for inherent qualities: no hay nada raro en eso (there's nothing strange about that).
02
A veces, por las tardes, yo salía a verlo y lo encontraba en el jardín junto al oratorio, sentado, absolutamente sereno, en un banco de piedra, y yo le contaba mis problemas, las dificultades que tenía con los esclavos, la desconfianza que me inspiraban el capataz, el tiempo o mis corredores de bolsa... todos los problemas que constituían lo largo y lo ancho de mi existencia.
Sometimes in the evenings I would go out to see him and find him in the garden beside the chapel, sitting perfectly still and serene on a stone bench, and I would tell him my problems — the difficulties with the slaves, the distrust I felt toward the foreman, the weather, my stockbrokers... all the problems that made up the length and breadth of my existence.
vocabulario
salir a verloSalir a + infinitive = to go out to do something. Salir signals outward movement with purpose: salió a buscar comida (went out for food), salió a dar un paseo (went out for a walk).
la desconfianza que me inspirabanInspirar used with emotions: to inspire, produce, provoke a feeling. Me inspira confianza (inspires trust in me), me inspira miedo (fills me with fear). Inspirar here = to produce an emotional response.
lo largo y lo anchoThe length and breadth — fixed expression. A lo largo y ancho de = throughout, all across: a lo largo y ancho del mundo (the world over). Here it's a metaphor for the full extent of his life.
corredores de bolsaStockbrokers — corredor = runner/broker (from correr). Corredor de bolsa is the fixed term. Bolsa = stock market (lit. purse/bag).
gramática
03
Y él escuchaba, haciendo sólo unos pocos comentarios, siempre comprensivo, de modo que cuando lo dejaba tenía la nítida impresión de que me había resuelto todos los problemas. No creía que pudiera negarle nada y prometí que, por mucho que me destrozara el corazón perderlo, cuando llegara el momento podría ingresar en el sacerdocio. Naturalmente, estaba equivocado.
And he would listen, making only a few comments, always understanding, so that when I left him I had the distinct impression that he had resolved all my problems. I didn't think I could refuse him anything, and I promised that however much it broke my heart to lose him, when the time came he could enter the priesthood. Naturally, I was wrong.
vocabulario
de modo queSo that — introduces a result or consequence. Synonym: de manera que, así que. More formal than así que in narrative.
la nítida impresiónNítido = sharp, clear, distinct. Used of images, sounds, memories: un recuerdo nítido (a vivid memory), una imagen nítida (a sharp image). More precise and visual than plain claro.
destrozar el corazónTo break someone's heart — stronger than romper el corazón. Destrozar implies shattering, not just breaking. Used for intense heartbreak.
ingresar enTo enter, join (an institution or order) — ingresar en el ejército (join the army), ingresar en el hospital (be admitted to hospital), ingresar en la universidad (enroll in university).
gramática
  • No creía que pudiera negarle nada — Negated creer triggers subjunctive: no creía que pudiera. In positive: creía que podía (indicative). A reliable rule from c01.
  • por mucho que me destrozara el corazón — Por mucho que + subjunctive: 'however much', 'no matter how much.' Productive construction: por más que insistas (no matter how much you insist), por mucho que llores (however much you cry).
§ Las visiones
04 💬 Muchacho / Vampiro / Muchacho
subjuntivo
El vampiro se detuvo en su relato. Por un momento, el chico siguió mirándolo y luego se sobresaltó como si acabara de despertar de un pensamiento profundo, y vaciló como si no pudiera encontrar las palabras adecuadas. —Ah..., ¿no quería ser sacerdote? —preguntó. El vampiro lo estudió como tratando de discernir el significado de su expresión.
The vampire paused in his telling. For a moment the boy kept watching him, then startled as if just rousing from a deep thought, and faltered as if he couldn't find the right words. "Ah... didn't he want to be a priest?" he asked. The vampire studied him as if trying to make out the meaning of his expression.
vocabulario
detenerse en su relatoTo pause in his account — detenerse = to stop, halt. Relato = account, narrative (more literary than historia).
se sobresaltóStartled — sobresaltarse = to start, be startled (lit. to jump over). A sudden involuntary reaction. Compare asustarse (to be frightened) — sobresaltarse is more physical and momentary.
vacilóFaltered, hesitated — vacilar = to hesitate, waver. Note: colloquially in Latin America vacilar = to mess around, joke. ¿Me estás vacilando? = Are you messing with me?
discernirTo discern, distinguish, make out — literary/formal. More precise than entender: implies active effort to perceive or distinguish.
gramática
  • como si acabara de despertar — Como si + imperfect subjunctive, as always. Note acabar de + infinitive = to have just done something. Acabara de (subjunctive) = as if he had just; acababa de (indicative) = had just.
05
Luego dijo: —Quiero decir que yo estaba equivocado con respecto a mí mismo, con no negarle nada. —Sus ojos se movieron hacia la pared lejana y se fijaron en los cristales de la ventana—. Empezó a tener visiones. —¿Visiones de verdad? —preguntó el muchacho, pero nuevamente su voz vaciló como si estuviera pensando en otra cosa.
Then he said: "I mean that I was wrong about myself — about not refusing him anything." His eyes moved toward the far wall and fixed on the windowpanes. "He began to have visions." "Real visions?" asked the boy, but again his voice faltered as if he were thinking of something else.
vocabulario
con respecto aWith respect to, regarding — formal phrase introducing a topic. Synonyms: en cuanto a, respecto de, en lo que se refiere a. All interchangeable in formal/written register.
fijarse enTo fix on, to notice — fijarse en = to fix one's gaze on, to notice, pay attention to. Very common: ¿te fijaste en eso? (did you notice that?). Fíjate as a discourse marker = look, notice, can you believe it.
de verdadReal, really — visiones de verdad = real visions. As a standalone: ¿de verdad? = really? A very common colloquial marker of surprise or confirmation.
gramática
06 💬 Vampiro
subjuntivo
—No lo pensé así —contestó el vampiro—. Sucedió cuando tenía quince años. Entonces él ya era muy hermoso. Tenía la piel más suave y los ojos azules más grandes. Era robusto, no delgado como yo soy ahora y era entonces... pero sus ojos... era como si cuando miraba sus ojos estuviese solo en el borde del mundo... en una playa de océano batida por el viento.
"I didn't see it that way," answered the vampire. "It happened when he was fifteen. He was already very beautiful then. He had the softest skin and the largest blue eyes. He was sturdy — not thin as I am now and was then... but his eyes... it was as if when I looked into his eyes I were alone at the edge of the world... on a windswept beach.
vocabulario
no lo pensé asíI didn't see it that way — pensar así = to think/see it that way. Pensar used for attitudes and views, not just cognition.
robustoSturdy, solidly built — of a person: stocky/solid; of things: strong and durable. More literary than fuerte for describing body type.
batida por el vientoWindswept — batir = to beat, lash. Batido por = beaten/lashed by. More evocative than azotado por: batido por las olas (lashed by the waves).
gramática
  • era como si... estuviese solo — Como si + imperfect subjunctive. Estuviese is an equally valid variant of estuviera — both imperfect subjunctive forms are interchangeable.
07
No había nada salvo el suave rugido de las olas. Pues bien —dijo, con sus ojos todavía fijos en los cristales de la ventana—, empezó a tener visiones. Al principio, sólo lo insinuaba, y dejó de comer completamente. Vivía en el oratorio. A cualquier hora, de día o de noche, podía encontrarlo en los desnudos lajares arrodillado frente al altar. Y el propio oratorio estaba descuidado.
There was nothing but the soft roar of the waves. "Well," he said, his eyes still fixed on the windowpanes, "he began to have visions. At first he only hinted at it, and he stopped eating entirely. He lived in the chapel. At any hour of the day or night, you could find him on the bare stone flags kneeling before the altar. And the chapel itself was neglected.
vocabulario
pues bienWell then, so then — discourse marker signaling resumption or conclusion. Pues alone = well/so; pues bien adds deliberate continuation. Common in formal speech and narrative.
al principioAt first — marks the early stage of something. Distinct from en primer lugar (firstly, in an argument). Al principio is the everyday colloquial form.
insinuabaHinted at it — insinuar = to hint, suggest indirectly. Insinuarse = to make advances (romantic). Insinuar algo without a direct object = to drop hints.
el propio oratorioThe chapel itself — propio as an intensifier: the very thing itself. El propio vampiro (the vampire himself), la propia ciudad (the city itself). More emphatic than mismo.
gramática
08
Dejó de atender las velas, de cambiar los manteles del altar o incluso de barrer las hojas. Una noche me alarmé de verdad cuando lo observé en el cenador de rosas durante una hora entera sin que se moviera de sus rodillas ni bajara ni una sola vez los brazos, que sostenía extendidos en forma de cruz. Todos los esclavos creían que estaba loco. —El vampiro arqueó las cejas con perplejidad—.
He stopped tending the candles, changing the altar cloths, or even sweeping the leaves. One night I was truly alarmed when I watched him in the rose bower for a whole hour without his stirring from his knees or once lowering his arms, which he held stretched out in the shape of a cross. All the slaves thought he had gone mad. The vampire arched his eyebrows in puzzlement.
vocabulario
arquear las cejasTo arch/raise the eyebrows — arquear = to arch, curve. The natural collocation for this gesture. Compare fruncir el ceño (to frown).
en forma de cruzIn the shape of a cross — en forma de = in the shape/form of. Fixed preposition phrase: en forma de V, en forma de arco.
gramática
  • sin que se moviera — Sin que + subjunctive when subjects differ: without his stirring (he = different subject). If same subject: sin moverse (infinitive). Compare: se fue sin decir nada (same subject) vs. se fue sin que nadie lo viera (different subjects).
09
Yo estaba convencido de que era simplemente demasiado celoso. Que en su amor a Dios había ido quizá demasiado lejos. Entonces me contó las visiones. Tanto Santo Domingo como la Santísima Virgen María se le habían aparecido en el oratorio. Le habían dicho que debía vender todas las propiedades que teníamos en Luisiana, todo lo que poseíamos, y usar el dinero para hacer la obra de Dios en Francia.
I was convinced that he was simply too devout — that in his love of God he had perhaps gone too far. Then he told me about the visions. Both Saint Dominic and the Most Holy Virgin Mary had appeared to him in the chapel. They had told him he must sell all the property we owned in Louisiana, everything we possessed, and use the money to do God's work in France.
vocabulario
tanto... comoBoth... and — inclusive pairing. Tanto X como Y = both X and Y. More formal than plain X e Y.
aparecerseTo appear (in a supernatural/religious sense) — aparecer = neutral; aparecerse (reflexive) specifically used for visions, ghosts, religious apparitions: se le apareció la Virgen (the Virgin appeared to him).
hacer la obra de DiosTo do God's work — obra = work, deed, labor; also a literary/artistic work, a construction project, a theater play. Hacer la obra is a formal religious phrase.
gramática
10
Mi hermano iba a ser un gran líder religioso, retornar el país a su antiguo fervor, cambiar el rumbo contra el ateísmo y la Revolución. Por supuesto, no tenía dinero propio. Yo debía vender las plantaciones y nuestras casas particulares en Nueva Orleans y darle el dinero a él.
My brother was to become a great religious leader, return the country to its old fervor, turn the tide against atheism and the Revolution. Of course, he had no money of his own. I was to sell the plantations and our private houses in New Orleans and give the money to him.
vocabulario
cambiar el rumboTo change course, turn the tide — rumbo = heading, course (nautical origin). Cambiar el rumbo = to change direction. Used figuratively for historical or political direction.
casas particularesPrivate homes — particular = private, personal. Casa particular = private residence. En particular = in particular; un asunto particular = a private matter.
gramática
§ La risa — la caída
11 💬 Muchacho
Una vez más el vampiro hizo una pausa. Y el muchacho quedó inmóvil, mirándolo, perplejo. —Ah..., perdóneme —susurró—. ¿Qué hizo? ¿Vendió las plantaciones?
Once again the vampire paused. And the boy sat motionless, watching him, perplexed. "Ah... forgive me," he whispered. "What did you do? Did you sell the plantations?"
vocabulario
una vez másOnce again — otra vez is nearly identical and more colloquial. De nuevo is slightly more formal.
quedó inmóvilRemained motionless — quedarse inmóvil = to freeze, stay perfectly still. The quedarse + adjective resultative construction again (cf. quedó perplejo, c01).
gramática
12 💬 Vampiro
subjuntivo
—No —dijo el vampiro, y su rostro estaba sereno como desde el principio—. Me reí de él. Y él se encolerizó. Insistía en que su mandato provenía de la propia Virgen. ¿Quién era yo para desacatarlo? ¿Quién? —preguntó en voz queda, como si volviera a pensarlo—. ¿Quién, en efecto?
"No," said the vampire, his face as serene as from the beginning. "I laughed at him. And he flew into a rage. He insisted that his mandate came from the Virgin herself. Who was I to defy it? Who?" he asked in a hushed voice, as if thinking it over again. "Who, indeed?"
vocabulario
reírse deTo laugh at, mock — reírse de alguien = to laugh at someone (dismissively). Distinct from reírse con alguien (to laugh along with someone).
se encolerizóFlew into a rage — literary; everyday: se enfureció, se puso hecho una furia. From cólera (cholera/rage).
provenir deTo come from, originate from — more formal/literary than venir de. Proveniente de = originating from (formal contexts).
desacatarTo defy, show contempt for (authority/orders) — more formal than desobedecer. Desacato = contempt (of court), insubordination.
en efectoIndeed, in fact — confirms or elaborates what was just said. Slightly more formal than de hecho. Used rhetorically to underscore a point.
gramática
  • ¿Quién era yo para desacatarlo? — Rhetorical ¿quién soy/era yo para...?: 'Who am/was I to do X?' Implies humility or acknowledgment of powerlessness. Natural in Spanish: ¿quién eres tú para decirme eso? (who are you to tell me that?).
13
Eran tonterías, le dije; el producto de una mente inmadura e incluso mórbida. El oratorio era un error; lo haría demoler de inmediato. Él iría a la escuela en Nueva Orleans y se le quitarían esas insensateces de la cabeza. No recuerdo todo lo que le dije. Pero sí recuerdo la sensación. Detrás de todo ese desprecio mío había una ira latente y una decepción. Estaba amargamente decepcionado.
It was all nonsense, I told him — the product of an immature and even morbid mind. The chapel was a mistake; I would have it demolished at once. He would go to school in New Orleans and have that foolishness knocked out of his head. I don't remember everything I said. But I do remember the feeling. Behind all that contempt of mine lay a latent anger and a disappointment. I was bitterly disappointed.
vocabulario
tonteríasNonsense, foolishness — from tonto (fool). ¡No digas tonterías! (don't talk nonsense). Una tontería can also mean a trifle: fue una tontería (it was nothing).
lo haría demolerHacer + infinitive causative: to have something done. Haría (conditional) = I would (past intention). Cf. haz funcionar las cintas from c01.
quitarle una idea de la cabezaTo knock an idea out of someone's head, talk them out of it — very common collocation. The opposite direction: se me metió en la cabeza (it got into my head).
amargamente decepcionadoBitterly disappointed — amargamente from amargo (bitter). Common pairing: amargamente arrepentido (bitterly sorry). Amargo can be literal (taste) or figurative.
gramática
14
No le creía en absoluto.
I didn't believe him in the slightest.
vocabulario
en absolutoNot at all, in the slightest — important: en absoluto alone (without no) already means 'absolutely not.' ¿Te molesta? — En absoluto. = Not at all. A common source of confusion for learners.
gramática
15 💬 Muchacho / Vampiro
subjuntivo condicional
—Pero eso es comprensible —dijo rápidamente el muchacho cuando el vampiro hizo una pausa—. Quiero decir, ¿alguien le habría creído? —¿Es tan comprensible? —el vampiro miró al entrevistador—. Pienso que tal vez haya sido un egoísmo vicioso. Déjame explicarte. Amaba a mi hermano y a veces creía que era un santo viviente.
"But that's understandable," the boy said quickly when the vampire paused. "I mean — would anyone have believed him?" "Is it so understandable?" The vampire looked at the interviewer. "I think it may have been a vicious selfishness. Let me explain. I loved my brother, and sometimes I thought he was a living saint."
vocabulario
comprensible vs. comprensivoEasy to confuse: comprensible = understandable, logical. Comprensivo = understanding, empathetic (a personality trait). Es muy comprensivo (he's very understanding) vs. es muy comprensible (it's very understandable).
egoísmo viciosoVicious selfishness — vicioso = vicious, depraved; also addicted. Stronger than malo — implies a moral failing rooted in character.
un santo vivienteA living saint — viviente = living (literary/formal). More solemn than vivo in this context.
gramática
  • tal vez haya sido — Tal vez/quizás + subjunctive: speaker uses subjunctive to express doubt. Indicative is also possible but signals more certainty: tal vez fue (it probably was) vs. tal vez haya sido (it may have been — more tentative).
16
Lo alentaba en sus rezos y meditaciones y estaba dispuesto a perderlo para que ingresara en el sacerdocio. Y si alguien me hubiera hablado de un santo en Arlés o en Lourdes que veía visiones, le habría creído. Era católico; creía en los santos. Encendía velas ante sus estatuas de mármol en las iglesias; conocía sus imágenes, sus símbolos, sus nombres. Pero no podía creerle a mi hermano.
I encouraged him in his prayers and meditations and was prepared to lose him to the priesthood. And if someone had told me about a saint in Arles or Lourdes who was having visions, I would have believed them. I was Catholic; I believed in saints. I lit candles before their marble statues in the churches; I knew their images, their symbols, their names. But I couldn't believe my own brother.
vocabulario
alentarTo encourage, hearten — from aliento (breath, spirit). More literary than animar. Alentar a alguien = to encourage someone. Also: to fan a flame, breathe life into.
encender velasEncender = to light, to turn on. Covers both igniting (encender una vela) and switching on (encender la luz/la tele).
gramática
  • si alguien me hubiera hablado... le habría creído — Past counterfactual: si + pluperfect subjunctive (hubiera hablado) → conditional perfect (habría creído). Standard structure for 'if X had happened, Y would have happened.'
17
No sólo no creía que viera visiones, era incapaz de entretener la noción ni por un instante. ¿Ahora bien, por qué? Porque era mi hermano. Santo podía ser, peculiar sin ninguna duda; pero ¿Francisco de Asís? No. No mi hermano. No podía ser así ningún hermano mío. Eso es egoísmo. ¿Lo ves?
Not only did I not believe he was having visions — I was incapable of entertaining the notion for even an instant. Now, why? Because he was my brother. He could be saintly, peculiar without a doubt — but Francis of Assisi? No. Not my brother. No brother of mine could be like that. That is selfishness. Do you see?
vocabulario
entretener la nociónTo entertain the notion — entretener = to entertain in all senses, including to hold an idea in one's mind. Entretener una idea = to let it stay, to turn it over.
ahora bienNow then, however — a discourse pivot. Introduces a clarification or contrast: 'that said, however.' More formal than plain pero. Common in explanations and arguments.
ni por un instanteNot for even an instant — ni as emphatic negation absorbs the negation: stronger than no... ni siquiera por un instante.
gramática
18 💬 Vampiro / Muchacho / Vampiro
El entrevistador lo pensó antes de contestar y entonces asintió con la cabeza y dijo que sí, que creía que sí. —Quizá tenía visiones —dijo el vampiro. —¿Entonces usted no afirma saber... ahora... si las tenía o no? —No, pero sé muy bien que jamás vaciló un segundo en sus convicciones. Eso lo sé y lo sabía entonces la noche que salió de mi cuarto enloquecido y apesadumbrado.
The interviewer thought before answering, then nodded and said yes, he thought so. "Perhaps he was having visions," said the vampire. "So you don't claim to know now whether he was or not?" "No. But I know very well that he never wavered for a second in his convictions. That I know, and knew it then, the night he left my room, maddened and grief-stricken."
vocabulario
asintió con la cabezaNodded — asentir = to assent, agree. Con la cabeza specifies the nodding gesture. Compare sacudir la cabeza (to shake one's head in negation).
enloquecido y apesadumbradoMaddened and grief-stricken — enloquecido from loco; apesadumbrado from pesadumbre (grief, sorrow). Both are elevated/literary words for extreme emotional states.
gramática

He didn't hesitate for even an instant. And within a matter of minutes he was dead.
vocabulario
en cuestión deIn a matter of — en cuestión de minutos/horas = in a matter of minutes/hours. Also: el asunto en cuestión (the matter in question/at issue).

subjuntivo
—¿Cómo? —preguntó el entrevistador. —Simplemente traspasó las puertas vidrieras, salió a la galería y se quedó un momento en lo alto de la escalera de ladrillos. Y entonces cayó. "How?" asked the interviewer. "He simply went through the glass doors, stepped out onto the gallery, and stood for a moment at the top of the brick stairs. And then he fell. "No, but two of the servants saw it."
vocabulario
traspasarTo go through, pass through — also: to transfer (a business), to pierce. Traspasar un límite = to overstep a boundary.
lo alto deThe top/upper part of — lo + adjective creates a noun: lo alto (the top), lo profundo (the depths), lo mejor (the best part).
con consternaciónWith dismay — consternado = dismayed. More formal than sorprendido or horrorizado — carries a sense of being troubled beyond words.
perder pieTo lose one's footing — also figurative: to get out of one's depth, lose one's grip on a situation. Compare meter la pata (to put one's foot in it).
gramática
21
Dijeron que había levantado los ojos como si acabara de ver algo en el aire. Luego todo su cuerpo se movió hacia adelante como si fuera barrido por un viento. Uno de ellos dijo que estaba a punto de decir algo cuando cayó. Yo también creí que estaba a punto de decir algo, pero fue en ese momento cuando me aparté de la ventana. Tenía la espalda vuelta cuando oí el ruido.
They said he had looked up as if he had just seen something in the air. Then his whole body moved forward as if swept by a wind. One of them said he was about to say something when he fell. I too thought he was about to say something, but it was at that moment that I stepped away from the window. My back was turned when I heard the sound.
vocabulario
estar a punto deTo be about to, on the verge of — estar a punto de + infinitive. Very common: estaba a punto de salir (I was just about to leave). A punto alone = just right (of food): el arroz está a punto.
apartarse deTo step away from, move back — apartarse de = to distance oneself from (literal or figurative): se apartó del grupo (he distanced himself from the group).
barrido por un vientoSwept by a wind — barrer = to sweep. Used of wind, water, and figuratively: barrió con todo (swept everything away).

§ La culpa — la caída en el vicio
22 💬 Muchacho / Vampiro
subjuntivo
—Pero, ¿cómo pudieron pensarlo? Usted dijo que hubo gente que lo vio caer. —No fue una acusación directa. Simplemente, sabían que algo desagradable había sucedido entre nosotros. Que habíamos discutido minutos antes de la caída. Los sirvientes nos habían oído, mi madre nos había oído.
"But how could they think that? You said there were people who saw him fall." "It wasn't a direct accusation. They simply knew that something unpleasant had happened between us — that we had argued minutes before the fall. The servants had heard us, my mother had heard us."
vocabulario
gramática
  • ¿cómo pudieron pensarlo? — Preterite of poder: 'how could they have thought that?' — expressing disbelief at what was possible on that occasion. The preterite focuses on the specific moment, not a general ability.
