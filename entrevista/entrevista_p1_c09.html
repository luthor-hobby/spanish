Pasaje 9

Primera parte — Pasaje 9

El esclavo Daniel descubre a Lestat enseñando los colmillos. Louis lo mata. El padre de Lestat pide perdón: Louis se hace pasar por Lestat en la oscuridad. Lestat llora y baila de rabia pero finalmente se arrodilla y perdona a su padre. Louis desangra al anciano para que no sufra. Lestat ataca a los esclavos. Louis incendia Pointe du Lac. Huyen a la plantación Freniere, donde Babette les da refugio.

§ El esclavo — tuve que matarlo
01 💬 Vampiro / Muchacho / Vampiro
subjuntivo imperativo
El vampiro se detuvo y se le movían los dos hombros con una risa silenciosa. Miraba al muchacho. Y el muchacho había mirado ya fijamente la boca del vampiro. —Te puedes imaginar —dijo el vampiro— lo que eso significaba. Tuve que matarlo. —¿Que tuvo qué? —dijo el muchacho. —Tuve que matar al esclavo. Empezó a correr. Hubiera alarmado a todos los demás.
The vampire stopped, his shoulders moving with a silent laugh. He was looking at the boy. And the boy had already stared fixedly at the vampire's mouth. 'You can imagine,' said the vampire, 'what that meant. I had to kill him.' 'You had to what?' said the boy. 'I had to kill the slave. He started to run. He would have alarmed all the others.'

Tenemos que salir de aquí. ¡Y mira lo que has hecho matando a éste! No hay tiempo que perder. —Eso era cierto. Lestat tenía razón. Yo podía oír a los esclavos reuniéndose en la cabaña de Daniel, esperándolo.
'I know he's my father!' said Lestat. 'That's why I can't kill him! I can't kill him! If I could, I would have done it long ago. We have to get out of here. And look what you've done by killing this one! There's no time to lose.' That was true. Lestat was right. I could hear the slaves gathering in Daniel's cabin, waiting for him.

gramática
  • Si pudiera, lo habría hecho — Conditional perfect with subjunctive condition: 'if I could' (hypothetical past) 'I would have done it' (result in past). Expresses unrealized past possibility.
  • no puedo matarlo — Present indicative: current inability, emotional rather than physical.

§ El padre perdona — Louis en la oscuridad
07 💬 Anciano
subjuntivo oxymoron
Estaba sentado, inclinado hacia adelante, hablándole a Lestat, rogándole a Lestat que le contestara, diciéndole que entendía su amargura mejor que el propio Lestat. Era un cadáver viviente. Nada animaba su cuerpo hundido sino una voluntad feroz: de ahí que sus ojos, por su brillo, estuvieran aún más hundidos en su cráneo, y sus labios en su temblor hicieran más horrible su vieja y amarillada boca.
He was sitting, leaning forward, speaking to Lestat, begging Lestat to answer him, telling him that he understood his bitterness better than Lestat himself. He was a living corpse. Nothing animated his sunken body but a fierce will: hence his eyes, for their brightness, were all the more sunken in his skull, and his lips in their trembling made all the more horrible his old and yellowed mouth.
vocabulario

gramática
  • Nada... sino una voluntad — Nada... sino construction: 'nothing... but' emphasizes what alone is responsible.
  • de ahí que... estuvieran... hicieran — Subjunctive after consequence marker: 'de ahí que' (hence/therefore) requires subjunctive in the dependent clauses.
  • sus labios en su temblor hicieran — Absolute participial phrase: 'his lips in their trembling' acts as independent modifier of the main clause.

It was not Lestat's voice, it was mine—a soft whisper. But he calmed down immediately, and I thought then perhaps he might die. But he held my hand as if dark oceanic waves were dragging him down and I were the only one who could save him. 'Lestat,' he said, 'for once, do not be cruel to me. For once, be for me the child you were. My son.'
