Entrevista con el Vampiro

Anne Rice · Capítulo 4: Primera parte

Pasaje 4

Primera parte — Pasaje 4

Los dos tambores: el corazón de Louis y el de Lestat. Louis bebe de la muñeca de Lestat. Ve como vampiro por primera vez: los colores, los botones de la chaqueta de Lestat, la luna. Su cuerpo humano muere mientras está plenamente vivo como vampiro. Lestat lo mete en el ataúd. El padre ciego de Lestat reclama su rosario. El muchacho pregunta sobre las cruces, los cerrojos, las estacas — todo desmentido. Lestat enciende el cigarrillo del muchacho.

§ Ver como vampiro — la luna
01 💬 Lestat
—El resultado fue que al cabo de unos minutos, yo estaba paralizado por la debilidad. Aterrorizado, descubrí que no podía siquiera hacer que hablara mi voluntad. Lestat seguía sosteniéndome, y su brazo era como el peso de una barra de hierro. Sentí que sus dientes se retiraban con tal nitidez que las dos heridas de punción parecían enormes, revestidas de dolor.
"The result was that after a few minutes I was paralyzed by weakness. Terrified, I discovered I couldn't even make my will speak. Lestat kept holding me, and his arm was like the weight of an iron bar. I felt his teeth withdraw with such precision that the two puncture wounds seemed enormous, lined with pain."
vocabulario
al cabo deafter (a period of time) — al cabo de unos minutos = after a few minutes; al cabo de + time = once that time has elapsed
hacer que hablara mi voluntadto make my will speak — causative hacer que + subjunctive; Louis means he couldn't even assert a thought or intention
con tal nitidezwith such precision/clarity — nitidez = sharpness, clarity; con tal X que sets up a consecutive (though here the result clause is implicit)
heridas de punciónpuncture wounds — herida = wound; punción = puncture; medical compound collocation
revestidas de dolorlined with pain — revestir = to line, to coat; a tactile image of pain as a surface sensation surrounding the wounds
gramática
  • no podía siquiera hacer que hablara mi voluntad — Siquiera in negative clauses: 'not even.' Ni siquiera / siquiera after a negative verb intensifies the impossibility.
  • hacer que hablara — Hacer que + subjuntivo: causative construction. 'To make (it) speak.' The subjunctive is required after hacer que.
02
Y ahora se inclinó sobre mi cabeza indefensa y se mordió la muñeca. La sangre fluyó sobre mi camisa y abrigo, y él la miraba con un ojo estrecho y reluciente. Me apretó la muñeca sangrante contra la boca. —Louis, bebe. —Y lo hice. —Constante, Louis — y — Aprisa —me susurró varias veces.
And now he bent over my defenseless head and bit his own wrist. The blood flowed over my shirt and coat, and he watched it with a narrow, gleaming eye. He pressed his bleeding wrist against my mouth. "Louis, drink." And I did. "Steady, Louis" — and — "Quickly" — he whispered to me several times.
vocabulario
indefensodefenseless, helpless — in- + defensa; Louis can't move or resist
con un ojo estrechowith a narrow eye, squinting — ojo estrecho = a narrowed, watchful gaze; implies sharp, calculating attention
apretar contrato press against — apretar = to press, to squeeze; apretó contra la boca: pressed it to his mouth
aprisaquickly, rapidly — an adverb; somewhat literary/formal, often found in older or elevated prose; synonym of rápido, deprisa
gramática
  • Y lo hice. — Proforma verbal con hacer: 'And I did.' Lo hice = I did it. Hacer as a pro-verb replacing the previous action (drinking) — extremely common in spoken and written Spanish.
03
Bebí, chupando la sangre de las heridas, experimentando por primera vez desde la infancia el placer especial de succionar alimento, el cuerpo enfocado con la mente en una fuente vital única. Y entonces sucedió algo.
I drank, sucking the blood from the wounds, experiencing for the first time since childhood the special pleasure of suckling nourishment, the body focused with the mind on a single vital source. And then something happened.
vocabulario
chuparto suck, to suckle — more physical/visceral than beber; implies active suction
por primera vez desdefor the first time since — a key temporal collocation combining por primera vez (for the first time) with desde (since)
fuente vitalvital source — fuente = source (also fountain); vital = life-giving; the body reduced to pure instinct
sucedió algosomething happened — suceder here is impersonal and ominous; signals a turning point without revealing what
gramática
  • el cuerpo enfocado con la mente en una fuente vital única — Participio en construcción absoluta: 'the body focused with the mind on a single vital source.' An absolute phrase — subject + participle — describes a simultaneous state without a conjugated verb.
04 💬 Vampiro
subjuntivo
—Qué patético resulta describir cosas que verdaderamente no pueden describirse —dijo, con la voz reducida casi a un susurro. El muchacho estaba sentado como si se hubiera petrificado. —Vi nada salvo esa luz mientras bebía sangre.
"How pathetic it is to try to describe things that truly cannot be described," he said, his voice reduced almost to a whisper. The boy sat as if he had turned to stone. "I saw nothing but that light as he drank."
vocabulario
qué patético resultahow pathetic it turns out to be — resultar + adjective = to prove/turn out to be; more evidential than es
con la voz reducida ahis voice reduced to — an absolute participial phrase; reducida = dropped, brought down
salvoexcept, but, save — formal/literary synonym of excepto
gramática
  • como si se hubiera petrificado — Como si + pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo: the pluperfect form signals the hypothetical event preceded the moment described — he sat as if he had (already) turned to stone.
05
Y luego Lestat retiró su muñeca de golpe, y abrí los ojos y me contuve en el momento en que me abalanzaba para agarrarla; me contuve porque me di cuenta de que el tambor era mi corazón, y el segundo tambor había sido el suyo. —Lo vi todo como un vampiro —dijo, con su voz ahora casi distante—. Lestat estaba al pie de la escalera y lo vi como no me había sido posible verlo antes.
And then Lestat pulled his wrist away sharply, and I opened my eyes and caught myself at the moment I was lunging for it; I caught myself because I realized that the drum was my heart, and the second drum had been his. "I saw everything as a vampire," he said, his voice now almost distant. "Lestat was at the foot of the stairs and I saw him as it had not been possible for me to see him before."
vocabulario
de golpesharply, all at once — de golpe = in one blow; marks sudden, abrupt action
contenerseto stop oneself, to hold back — reflexive; implies overcoming a powerful instinctive impulse
abalanzarseto lunge, to hurl oneself forward — sudden, uncontrolled movement driven by hunger or instinct
darse cuenta de queto realize that — one of the most essential collocations in Spanish
al pie de la escaleraat the foot of the stairs — spatial collocation: al pie de = at the base/foot of
gramática
  • como no me había sido posible verlo antes — Ser posible + infinitivo: impersonal construction. 'As it had not been possible for me to see him before.' Me is the dative of interest marking the experiencer.
06
Estaba radiante, no luminoso. Y entonces vi que no sólo Lestat había cambiado, sino todas las cosas.
He was radiant, not luminous. And then I saw that not only Lestat had changed, but all things.
vocabulario
radianteradiant — glowing with an inner light, implying warmth and intensity; different from luminoso which is simply light-emitting
no sólo... sinonot only... but (also) — the standard Spanish correlative for extension/addition
todas las cosasall things — a grand, philosophical register; not just todo but the enumerable things of the world
gramática
  • no sólo Lestat había cambiado, sino todas las cosas — No sólo... sino with ellipsis: the second clause omits the repeated verb (habían cambiado) for concision. 'Not only Lestat had changed, but all things [had].'
07 💬 Lestat / Vampiro
subjuntivo
—Fue como si fuera la primera vez que podía ver colores y formas. Estaba tan extasiado con los botones del abrigo negro de Lestat que no miré ninguna otra cosa durante mucho tiempo. Luego Lestat empezó a reírse, y oí su risa como nunca había oído nada. Todavía oía su corazón latir como el toque de un tambor, y ahora llegó esta risa metálica.
"It was as if it were the first time I could see colors and shapes. I was so entranced with the buttons on Lestat's black coat that I didn't look at anything else for a long time. Then Lestat began to laugh, and I heard his laughter as I had never heard anything. I could still hear his heart beating like the stroke of a drum, and now came this metallic laughter."
vocabulario
extasiado conentranced by, in rapture over — éxtasis = ecstasy; extasiado con = lost in wonder at
el toque de un tamborthe stroke/beat of a drum — toque = a stroke, ring, or beat; toque de queda = curfew; toque de difuntos = death knell
risa metálicametallic laughter — a synesthetic description; Lestat's laugh heard with vampire senses as having a hard, resonant, inhuman quality
gramática
  • como si fuera la primera vez que podía ver — Como si + imperfecto de subjuntivo: introduces a hypothetical comparison. Note: podía (imperfect indicative) inside the relative clause because it describes what was factually happening within the hypothetical.
  • como nunca había oído nada — Como + negación implícita: 'as I had never heard anything.' Nunca... nada double negative stacks for emphasis.
08
Era confuso, cada sonido fundiéndose en el próximo, como las reverberaciones imbricadas de las campanas, hasta que aprendí a separar los sonidos, y luego se solapaban, cada uno suave pero distinto, aumentando pero separado, repiques de campanas. —¡Deja de mirar mis botones! —dijo Lestat—. Vete a los árboles.
It was confusing, each sound merging into the next, like the overlapping reverberations of bells, until I learned to separate the sounds, and then they overlapped, each soft but distinct, swelling but separate, peals of bells. "Stop staring at my buttons!" said Lestat. "Go to the trees."
vocabulario
fundirse ento merge into, to blend into — fundirse = to melt, to fuse; fundiéndose en el próximo: each sound dissolving into the next
imbricadooverlapping, interlocking — from Latin; like overlapping roof tiles; formal/literary term
solaparseto overlap — solape = overlap; used of sounds, images, meanings that coincide or partially cover each other
repique de campanaspeal/ringing of bells — repique = a peal or chime; specifically the bright, rapid ringing of bells
dejar de + infinitivoto stop doing — deja de mirar = stop looking; dejar de marks cessation of an ongoing action
gramática
  • cada sonido fundiéndose en el próximo — Gerundio en construcción absoluta: 'each sound merging into the next.' The gerund describes a concurrent process without a main verb, creating an impressionistic, simultaneous texture.
09
¡Sácate de encima todos los desechos humanos de tu cuerpo, y no te enamores tan locamente de la noche que pierdas tu sentido! Ésa, por supuesto, fue una orden sabia. Cuando vi la luna sobre las piedras, me quedé tan enamorado de ella que debo de haber pasado una hora allí. Cuando regresé, mi cuerpo empezó a dolerme. Todos mis fluidos humanos estaban siendo expulsados de mí.
"Rid yourself of all the human waste from your body, and don't fall so madly in love with the night that you lose your senses!" That, of course, was a wise order. When I saw the moon on the stones, I fell so in love with it that I must have spent an hour there. When I returned, my body began to ache. All my human fluids were being expelled from me.
vocabulario
sacarse algo de encimato rid oneself of something — quitarse/sacarse de encima: to get rid of, to shake off; colloquial and vivid
enamorarse de la nocheto fall in love with the night — enamorarse de + abstract noun; used literally and figuratively for being captivated
debo de haber pasadoI must have spent — deber de + infinitive = probability/deduction about the past; note: deber de (probability) vs. deber (obligation)
fluidos humanoshuman fluids — the body voiding itself as it dies; Louis observes his own death with clinical detachment
perder el sentidoto lose one's senses/mind — perder el sentido = to lose consciousness or reason; here Lestat warns against vampiric rapture
gramática
  • no te enamores tan locamente de la noche que pierdas tu sentido — Tan... que + subjuntivo (consecutiva de finalidad negada): 'don't fall so madly in love with the night that you lose your senses.' The subjunctive in the result clause signals a potential/feared outcome, not an actual one.
  • estaban siendo expulsados — Pasiva perifrástica progresiva: estar siendo + past participle = progressive passive. Emphasizes the ongoing process of expulsion, not just the fact.
10
Morí como humano, pero completamente vivo como vampiro; y con mis sentidos despiertos, tuve que presidir la muerte de mi cuerpo con cierta incomodidad y luego, finalmente, miedo.
I died as a human, but was completely alive as a vampire; and with my senses awake, I had to preside over the death of my body with a measure of discomfort and then, finally, fear.
vocabulario
presidirto preside over, to oversee — formal verb; Louis watches his own bodily death as if chairing a meeting he cannot adjourn
con cierta incomodidadwith a measure of discomfort — cierta before a noun = a certain (degree of); softens the noun that follows
sentidos despiertossenses awake, senses alert — despierto = awake/alert; sentidos despiertos: full vampiric sensory awareness
gramática
  • tuve que presidir — Tener que + infinitivo = obligación concreta: 'had to preside.' Unlike deber (moral duty), tener que signals a concrete, inescapable necessity.
§ Morir como humano — el ataúd
11 💬 Lestat / Louis / Lestat / Louis / Vampiro
subjuntivo condicional
—¿Eres un hombre rico —me dijo cuando entré. —Algo me está sucediendo —grité. —Te estás muriendo, eso es todo; no seas tonto. ¿No tienes una lámpara de petróleo? Con todo ese dinero y no puedes permitirte aceite de ballena salvo para ese farol. Tráeme ese farol. —¡Me muero! —grité—. ¡Me muero! —Le pasa a todo el mundo —persistió negándose a ayudarme.
"You're a rich man," he said to me when I entered. "Something is happening to me," I cried. "You're dying, that's all; don't be foolish. Don't you have an oil lamp? With all that money and you can't afford whale oil except for that lantern. Bring me that lantern." "I'm dying!" I cried. "I'm dying!" "It happens to everyone," he persisted, refusing to help me.
vocabulario
eso es todothat's all — a dismissive phrase to minimize what has just been said: no seas tonto, eso es todo = don't be silly, that's all
no seas tontodon't be foolish — imperative of ser; tonto = fool, silly person; Lestat's contemptuous impatience
permitirseto afford, to permit oneself — no puede permitirse = can't afford; also used for luxuries: no me lo puedo permitir
le pasa a todo el mundoit happens to everyone — fixed expression; pasarle a alguien = to happen to someone; todo el mundo = everyone
persistir negándose ato persist in refusing to — persistir + gerund = to keep on doing; negarse a = to refuse to
gramática
  • te estás muriendo — Perífrasis progresiva (estar + gerundio): 'you are dying.' The progressive emphasizes the ongoing process — the death is happening right now, not just a general truth.
  • le pasa a todo el mundo — Construcción impersonal con dativo: pasar + indirect object. 'It happens to everyone.' Le marks the recipient of the event.
12
Cuando lo recuerdo, aún siento desprecio hacia él. No porque tuviera miedo, sino porque hubiera podido llamar mi atención sobre estos cambios con reverencia. Podía haberme calmado y habérselo hecho ver con la misma fascinación con que había visto y sentido la noche. Pero no lo hizo. Lestat nunca fue el vampiro que soy yo. Para nada. —El vampiro no lo dijo con jactancia.
When I remember it, I still feel contempt for him. Not because he was afraid, but because he could have drawn my attention to these changes with reverence. He could have calmed me and made me see it with the same fascination with which I had seen and felt the night. But he didn't. Lestat was never the vampire that I am. Not at all. The vampire did not say it with boastfulness.
vocabulario
desprecio haciacontempt for — desprecio = contempt, disdain; hacia marks the target of the feeling
no porque... sino porquenot because... but because — a key contrastive structure for correcting a misattribution
llamar la atención sobreto draw attention to — a very common collocation: llamar la atención sobre algo = to point out, to highlight
para nadanot at all — an emphatic negation; stronger than no alone; here stands as its own emphatic sentence
con jactanciawith boastfulness, boastingly — jactancia = bragging, conceit; jactarse de = to boast of
gramática
  • No porque tuviera miedo, sino porque hubiera podido — No porque + subjuntivo: 'Not because he was afraid.' No porque always takes the subjunctive — it denies a causal connection rather than asserting a fact.
  • Podía haberme calmado y habérselo hecho ver — Podía + infinitivo compuesto: 'could have calmed me.' The imperfect podía + perfect infinitive expresses an unrealized past ability.
13
Lo dijo como si de verdad hubiera preferido que fuera de otra manera.
He said it as if he truly would have preferred it to be otherwise.
vocabulario
de verdadtruly, really — an intensifier: de verdad = for real, genuinely; emphasizes sincerity
que fuera de otra manerafor it to be otherwise — de otra manera = differently, in another way; the subjunctive fuera follows preferir
gramática
  • como si de verdad hubiera preferido que fuera de otra manera — Como si + pluscuamperfecto / imperfecto de subjuntivo en cadena: two subjunctives nested. Como si hubiera preferido (hypothetical past wish) governs que fuera (hypothetical state). The whole clause is Louis reading Lestat's inner life.
14 💬 Lestat / Louis / Lestat / Vampiro / Lestat
—Alors —dijo con un suspiro—, me moría rápidamente; lo que significaba que mi capacidad de tener miedo estaba disminuyendo con igual rapidez. —¡Oh, por el amor del demonio! —empezó a gritar—. ¿Te das cuenta de que no he preparado ningún ataúd para ti? ¡Qué imbécil soy! Estuve tentado en decir: Pues lo eres, pero no dije nada. —Tendrás que acostarte conmigo esta mañana. No te he preparado un ataúd.
"Alors," he said with a sigh, "I was dying rapidly; which meant that my capacity for fear was diminishing with equal speed." "Oh, for the love of the devil!" he began to shout. "Do you realize I haven't prepared any coffin for you? What an idiot I am!" I was tempted to say: Yes you are, but I said nothing. "You'll have to lie down with me this morning. I haven't prepared a coffin for you."
vocabulario
alors(French) well then, so — Lestat's French affectation; a Gallicism signaling a shift of topic or a resigned acceptance
por el amor del demoniofor the love of the devil — a vampire's version of por el amor de Dios (for heaven's sake); comic and revealing
darse cuenta de queto realize that — essential collocation; ¿te das cuenta de que...? = do you realize that...?
estar tentado de/ento be tempted to — estar tentado de (standard) or tentado en (regional/older); signals a suppressed impulse
acostarse conto lie down with — here literal (sharing a coffin); not the usual sexual connotation
gramática
  • lo que significaba que — Lo que + verbo de consecuencia: 'which meant that.' Lo que = 'which' as a relative pronoun referring back to the entire previous clause.
  • Tendrás que acostarte conmigo — Tener que + infinitivo en futuro: future obligation. The future tense of tener makes this a command disguised as a statement.
15
El vampiro se rió. —La alusión al ataúd tocó una veta mía de terror que pienso que absorbió toda la capacidad de terror que me quedaba. Luego Lestat fue con su padre, diciéndole adiós, que regresaría por la mañana. —Pero ¿adónde vas, por qué tienes que vivir con semejante horario? —quería saber el anciano, y Lestat se impacientó. —¿Acaso no cuido de ti? —preguntó—.
The vampire laughed. "The allusion to the coffin struck a vein of terror in me that I think absorbed all the capacity for terror I had left. Then Lestat went to his father, saying goodbye, that he would return in the morning." "But where are you going, why do you have to keep such a schedule?" the old man wanted to know, and Lestat grew impatient. "Don't I take care of you?" he asked.
vocabulario
tocar una veta deto strike a vein of — veta = a vein or seam (in mining); here a deep, hidden reserve of terror, as if struck like ore
que me quedabathat I had left — quedar = to remain; que me quedaba = that remained to me
semejante horariosuch a schedule — semejante before a noun = such a, that kind of; always singular and before the noun
acasodon't (in rhetorical questions) — ¿acaso no...? = isn't it true that...? Marks a rhetorical question expecting a 'yes' answer
impacientarseto grow impatient — reflexive inchoative: se impacientó = became impatient (change of state)
gramática
  • ¿Acaso no cuido de ti? — Acaso en interrogativas retóricas: ¿acaso no...? = 'Don't I...?' It introduces a rhetorical question where the speaker expects the answer to be obvious.
16
¡Te he conseguido un techo mejor del que tú jamás pusiste sobre mi cabeza! Si quiero dormir todo el día y beber toda la noche, lo haré, ¡maldito seas!
I have gotten you a better roof than you ever put over my head! If I want to sleep all day and drink all night, I shall, damn you!
vocabulario
conseguir un techoto get/provide a roof — techo = roof, but also shelter/home; poner un techo sobre la cabeza = to put a roof over one's head
mejor del que tú jamás pusistebetter than you ever put — del que = comparison with clause; jamás = ever (in affirmative after a comparative = strongest negative)
maldito seasdamn you, curse you — ser + maldito in the subjunctive = a curse formula; maldito = damned/cursed
lo haréI shall (do it), I will — the emphatic future; lo refers back to sleeping all day and drinking all night
gramática
  • Si quiero... lo haré — Condicional real con futuro: 'If I want to... I will.' Present indicative in the if-clause + future in the main clause = a real (not hypothetical) condition. Lestat states this as a pure assertion of will.
  • maldito seas — Subjuntivo optativo/de deseo: a wish or curse formula. The present subjunctive expresses a strong desire or imprecation: 'may you be damned.'
17 💬 Anciano / Lestat
El anciano se puso a gemir. Sólo mi extraña sensación de agotamiento me impidió manifestar mi desaprobación. Miraba la escena a través de la puerta abierta, extasiado con los colores de la colcha y el positivo alboroto de colores en el rostro del anciano. Sus venas azules palpitaban bajo su carne rosa y grisácea.
The old man began to moan. Only my strange sensation of exhaustion prevented me from expressing my disapproval. I watched the scene through the open door, entranced with the colors of the quilt and the positive riot of colors in the old man's face. His blue veins throbbed beneath his pink and grayish flesh.
vocabulario
ponerse a + infinitivoto begin to, to start — se puso a gemir: started moaning; ponerse a marks a sudden onset of action
manifestar desaprobaciónto express disapproval — manifestar = to express, to show; formal and controlled, contrasting with the scene's rawness
alboroto de coloresriot of colors — alboroto = uproar, tumult; used figuratively for a vivid, overwhelming visual spectacle
palpar / palpitarto throb, to pulse — palpitar = to throb rhythmically; palpitar shares a root with pálpito (premonition) and palpación (palpation)
gramática
  • sólo mi extraña sensación de agotamiento me impidió — Sujeto enfático frontalizado: the subject is front-loaded and marked with sólo for emphasis. 'Only my strange sense of exhaustion prevented me.' Impedir takes an indirect object: me impidió manifestar.
18
Encontré apelables incluso el amarillo de sus dientes; y quedé casi hipnotizado por el temblor de su labio. —¡Semejante hijo, semejante hijo! —dijo, sin sospechar, por supuesto, la verdadera naturaleza de su hijo. —De acuerdo, pues, vete. Sé que tienes una mujer en alguna parte; vas a verla en cuanto su marido se va por las mañanas. Dame mi rosario. ¿Qué le ha pasado a mi rosario?
I found even the yellow of his teeth appealing; and I was left almost hypnotized by the trembling of his lip. "Such a son, such a son!" he said, not suspecting, of course, his son's true nature. "Very well, then, go. I know you have a woman somewhere; you go to see her as soon as her husband leaves in the mornings. Give me my rosary. What has happened to my rosary?"
vocabulario
apelableappealing, attractive — from apelar; here used as 'having visual appeal'; an unusual word choice that marks Louis's new sensory perception
quedar hipnotizadoto be left hypnotized, to end up mesmerized — quedar + past participle: resulting state
semejante hijosuch a son — semejante used exclamatively = what a, such a; here the old man's lament
de acuerdo, puesvery well then, alright then — de acuerdo = agreed/OK + pues = so/then; a resigned concession
¿qué le ha pasado a?what has happened to? — pasarle a algo/alguien = to happen to; the dative marks what has been affected
gramática
  • en cuanto su marido se va — En cuanto + presente de indicativo: 'as soon as her husband leaves.' When referring to a habitual/repeated action in the present, en cuanto takes the indicative.
19
Lestat dijo algo blasfemo y le entregó el rosario...
Lestat said something blasphemous and handed him the rosary...
vocabulario
algo blasfemosomething blasphemous — algo + adjective = something [adjective]; the content is left to the imagination
entregarto hand over, to deliver, to give — entregar implies a formal or deliberate handing-over; more specific than dar
el rosariothe rosary — the old man's clinging to religious objects while his son is a vampire; the dramatic irony the ellipsis leaves hanging
gramática
  • dijo algo blasfemo y le entregó... — Elipsis narrativa con puntos suspensivos: the sentence trails off with ellipsis, leaving the blasphemy and the moment unexpanded. Rice uses this to move quickly past Lestat and keep focus on Louis's observation.
§ Las cruces — el cigarrillo
20 💬 Muchacho / Lestat
—¿Usted se metió en su ataúd? —No tuve otra posibilidad. Le rogué a Lestat que me dejara quedar en el armario, pero se rió, asombrado. —¿No sabes lo que eres? —me preguntó—. Bien, ya te estás portando mal, y ya casi es el alba. Tan pronto como sea de noche puedo prepararte uno. Te dejaré morir primero si quieres.
"You got into his coffin?" "I had no other choice. I begged Lestat to let me stay in the wardrobe, but he laughed, astonished." "Don't you know what you are?" he asked me. "Look, you're already behaving badly, and it's almost dawn. As soon as it's night I can prepare one for you. I'll let you die first if you like."
vocabulario
no tuve otra posibilidadI had no other choice — posibilidad = possibility/option; no tener otra posibilidad = to have no alternative
