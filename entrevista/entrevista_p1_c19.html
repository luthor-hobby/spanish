Primera parte — Pasaje 19

Claudia quiere su propio ataúd. El fabricante de ataúdes — una tragedia en un acto. Louis llora por primera vez como vampiro. Claudia cambia: deja de matar indiscriminadamente. Patrones exigentes. Lestat la llama 'la muerte infantil, la hermana muerte'. Las sirvientas desaparecen — Claudia las mató. La paz de la casa queda destruida pero hay quietud. Lestat mello por el vino. Louis le dice: 'Ya todo ha terminado.'

§ El ataúd propio — el fabricante

Íbamos a ir a ver al fabricante de ataúdes. Una obra, una tragedia en un solo acto: yo la dejaría en su pequeño salón y confiaría al hombre en el antesala que ella iba a morir. Habla de amor, ella debe tener lo mejor, pero ella no debe saberlo; y el fabricante de ataúdes, sacudido por la tragedia de ello, debe hacerlo para ella. —Pero, ¿por qué..., Claudia? —le rogué yo.
We were going to see the coffin maker. A work, a tragedy in a single act: I would leave her in his small parlor and confide in the man in the antechamber that she was going to die. Speak of love, she must have the best, but she must not know it; and the coffin maker, shaken by the tragedy of it, must make it for her. "But why..., Claudia?" I begged her.

Detestaba hacer eso, detestaba jugar al gato y al ratón con el indefenso ser humano. —¿Pero por qué debe morir? —me preguntó, como si yo fuera el Dios que lo había dictaminado. —Su corazón... No puede vivir —dije, y las palabras cobraron en mí un poder peculiar, una afligida resonancia.
I hated doing that, hated playing cat and mouse with the helpless human being. "But why must he die?" she asked me, as if I were the God who had decreed it. "His heart... He cannot live," I said, and the words took on in me a peculiar power, an afflicted resonance.

Y allí estaba el ataúd en nuestro dormitorio, donde lo miraba a menudo durante horas cuando era nuevo, como si la cosa se moviera o estuviera viva o le revelara algún misterio poco a poco, como hacen las cosas que cambian. Pero no dormía en él. Dormía conmigo. Tuvo otros cambios. No mataba de forma indiscriminada. Caía en patrones exigentes. La pobreza empezó a fascinarla.
And there was the coffin in our bedroom, where I would look at it often for hours when it was new, as if the thing might move or be alive or reveal to me some mystery gradually, as things that change do. But she did not sleep in it. She slept with me. She had other changes. She did not kill indiscriminately. She fell into exacting patterns. Poverty began to fascinate her.

Las sirvientas habían desaparecido. Dos de las mejores criadas que habíamos tenido jamás, una madre y una hija. Cuando Lestat cerró la puerta al volver, fuera estaba el padre golpeando la aldaba. The servants had disappeared. Two of the best maids we had ever had, a mother and a daughter. When Lestat closed the door upon returning, outside was the father pounding the knocker. He told me they were nothing. But Lestat whispered to me: "It is her! She has done something to them. Lestat attacked the servants. He told me that he would go tell the world. Era una tarde temprana, cuando me acababa de despertar. La casa estaba oscura. It was an early evening, when I had just awakened. The house was dark. I saw her standing by the French doors; she wore puffed sleeves and a pink bow, and was looking with lowered eyelids at the evening rush on Rue Royale. "He will not tell me anything," she said softly. I had not realized that she knew that I had opened my eyes. "You will tell me, won't you? How was it done?"
