Primera parte — Pasaje 17

Lestat lleva la niña al hotel. Los cadáveres siguen allí. Lestat pone a Louis en un dilema: toma a la niña. Louis la besa y la muerde. Lestat le da su muñeca. La niña bebe y ya no puede parar. Claudia se convierte en vampira. Lestat la llama hija suya y de Louis. 'Ahora Louis iba a abandonarnos', dice Lestat. Louis furioso. La niña pregunta: '¿Dónde está mi mamá?'

Los cadáveres estaban tal cual los habíamos dejado; uno meticulosamente echado en el ataúd como si un enterrador ya lo hubiera atendido, el otro en su silla en la mesa. Lestat los esquivó como si no los viera, mientras yo lo observaba con fascinación. Las velas se habían consumido, y la única luz era la de la luna y la de la calle.
The corpses were just as we had left them; one meticulously laid out in the coffin as if an undertaker had already attended to it, the other in his chair at the table. Lestat avoided them as if he did not see them, while I watched him with fascination. The candles had burned down, and the only light was from the moon and the street.

04 💬 Lestat / Vampiro
Y lo hice. Me acerqué a la cama y la observé. El pecho apenas se le movía con su aliento, y una de sus manitas estaba enredada en su largo cabello dorado. La tomé en mis brazos y la sostuve, su mejilla ardiente sobre la mía, su pelo cayendo sobre mis muñecas y rozando mis párpados, el dulce perfume de una niña fuerte y pulsante a pesar de la enfermedad y la muerte.
And I did. I approached the bed and observed her. Her chest barely moved with her breathing, and one of her small hands was tangled in her long golden hair. I took her in my arms and held her, her burning cheek against mine, her hair falling over my wrists and brushing my eyelids, the sweet scent of a child strong and pulsating despite illness and death.

§ Claudia — nuestra hija

Y entonces Lestat me apartó de ella. —Pero no está muerta —susurré. Pero todo había terminado. Los muebles de la habitación emergieron de la oscuridad. Me senté atontado, mirándola, demasiado débil para moverme. Lestat la estaba cogiendo, hablándole, diciéndole un nombre. —Claudia, Claudia, escúchame; despierta, Claudia.
And then Lestat pulled me away from her. "But she is not dead," I whispered. But it was all over. The furniture of the room emerged from the darkness. I sat stunned, looking at her, too weak to move. Lestat was taking her, speaking to her, telling her a name. "Claudia, Claudia, listen to me; wake up, Claudia."

He was now carrying her from the bedroom into the parlor, and his voice was so soft I could barely hear it. "You are ill, do you hear me? You must do what I say to cure yourself." "Where is my mother?" asked the girl in a low voice. Her voice was equal to her physical beauty, clear as a silver bell. She was sensual. She was sensual. Her eyes were as wide and clear as Babette's. "She is our daughter," he said. "She will live with us."
