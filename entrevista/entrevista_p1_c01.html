Entrevista con el Vampiro

Anne Rice · Capítulo 1: Primera parte

Pasaje 1

Primera parte — Pasaje 1

San Francisco, años 70. Un joven periodista sube a un apartamento con su magnetófono. El vampiro cruza la habitación hacia la ventana. La luz de la calle lo ilumina desde atrás. Cuando encienden la lámpara, el muchacho ve por primera vez lo que tiene delante: blanco como hueso, ojos verdes como llamas. El vampiro quiere contar su historia. Empezó en 1791, en las plantaciones de Luisiana. Hubo una tragedia — su hermano menor.

§ La habitación — el equipo
01 💬 Vampiro
Ya veo... —dijo el vampiro, pensativo, y lentamente cruzó la habitación hacia la ventana. Durante largo rato, se quedó allí contra la luz mortecina de la calle Divisadero y los focos intermitentes del tránsito. El muchacho pudo ver entonces los muebles del cuarto con mayor claridad: la mesa redonda de roble, las sillas. Una palangana colgaba de una pared con un espejo.
"I see..." said the vampire pensively, and slowly crossed the room toward the window. For a long while he stayed there against the dying light of Divisadero Street and the flickering beams of traffic. The boy could then make out the room's furniture more clearly: the round oak table, the chairs. A washbasin hung on one wall with a mirror.
vocabulario
durante largo ratoRato = a span of time, often brief. Modified by largo it stretches into 'a good while.' Fixed collocations: al rato (a little while later), un buen rato (quite a while). Not interchangeable with tiempo in these phrases.
la luz mortecinaMortecino (from muerte) describes light or fire that is guttering out — fading, dying. Only used of light, flame, or color, never of people. More evocative than tenue (faint) or débil (weak).
con mayor claridadFormal/literary alternative to más claramente. Mayor before abstract nouns raises the register: con mayor facilidad, con mayor precisión. Everyday speech prefers más claro.
gramática
  • se quedó allí — Quedarse as positional stative: 'to remain, stay put.' Distinct from estar (neutral state) — quedarse implies a result or decision: he ended up standing there. Compare se fue (left) vs. se quedó (stayed).
  • pudo ver entonces — Preterite of poder = managed to, was able to (on this specific occasion). Imperfect podía ver would mean 'could see (generally).' The preterite marks the moment clarity became possible.

vocabulario
desde luegoOf course — stronger and more deliberate than claro (which can sound throwaway) or por supuesto. Desde luego signals firm, unhesitating assent.
sumamenteExtremely, utterly — a formal intensifier. In conversation you'd hear muy or súper; sumamente adds an elevated, almost ironic register here.
¿no le parece?Don't you think? — Parecer takes an indirect object (le) rather than a subject; the 'it' is implicit. A polite tag inviting agreement. ¿No te parece? in the tú form.
gramática
  • para que el muchacho pudiera verle el perfil — Para que + subjunctive: purpose clause required when subjects differ. Note verle el perfil: the indirect object le signals whose profile it is — Spanish uses the dative where English uses a possessive ('see his profile').

§ La luz — el vampiro revelado
05 💬 Vampiro / Muchacho / Vampiro / Muchacho
—No —dijo abruptamente el vampiro—. No podemos empezar de esa manera. ¿Tienes ya el equipo dispuesto? —Sí —dijo el muchacho. —Entonces, siéntate. Voy a encender la luz. —Yo pensaba que a los vampiros no les gustaba la luz —dijo el muchacho—. Si usted cree que la oscuridad ayuda al ambiente... —Pero en ese momento dejó de hablar. El vampiro lo miraba dando la espalda a la ventana.
"No," said the vampire abruptly. "We can't begin that way. Is your equipment ready?" "Yes," said the boy. "Then sit down. I'm going to turn on the light." "I thought vampires didn't like light," said the boy. "If you think the darkness helps the mood..." But just then he stopped. The vampire was watching him with his back to the window.
vocabulario
el equipo dispuestoDispuesto here = set up, ready, in place — not 'disposed' in the psychological sense. Tener algo dispuesto is a useful fixed phrase: to have something arranged and ready to go.
dar la espalda aTo have one's back to (literal) or to turn one's back on (figurative). Fixed expression: dar la espalda a alguien can mean to ignore or abandon someone — the physical sense and the social snub share the same phrase.
dejó de hablarDejar de + infinitive = to stop doing something. Implies the stopping was somewhat involuntary or situationally compelled, vs. parar de (more abrupt, colloquial) or cesar de (formal).
gramática
  • siéntate — Tú affirmative imperative of sentarse: stem-change e→ie. Reflexive imperatives drop the -s before attaching -os (vosotros): sentaos, not sentados.
06
El muchacho ahora no podía distinguir la cara e incluso había algo en su figura que lo distraía. Empezó a decir algo, pero no dijo nada. Y luego echó un suspiro de alivio cuando el vampiro se acercó a la mesa y extendió la mano al cordón de la luz.
The boy could no longer make out his face, and there was something about his silhouette that unsettled him. He started to say something, but said nothing. And then he let out a breath of relief when the vampire moved toward the table and reached for the light cord.
vocabulario
echar un suspiroEchar as a light verb in dozens of collocations: echar un vistazo (take a look), echar una mano (lend a hand), echar de menos (miss someone), echar un suspiro (heave a sigh). The verb itself contributes almost no meaning — the noun does the work.
extendió la manoTo reach out / extend one's hand — more formal or literary than agarrar or coger. Extender la mano can also mean to offer help or a handshake.
gramática
  • e incluso — Y becomes e before words beginning with i- or hi-: español e inglés, padre e hijo. Same rule flips o → u before o-/ho-: siete u ocho.
07 💬 Muchacho
De inmediato la habitación se inundó de una dura luz amarilla. Y el muchacho, mirando al vampiro, no pudo reprimir una exclamación. Sus dedos bailotearon por la mesa para asirse al borde. —¡Dios santo! —susurró, y luego, contempló, estupefacto, al vampiro.
At once the room flooded with harsh yellow light. And the boy, staring up at the vampire, couldn't hold back a gasp. His fingers scrambled back across the table to grip the edge. "Good God!" he whispered, and then he stared, dumbstruck, at the vampire.
vocabulario
de inmediatoImmediately, at once — more emphatic than inmediatamente. Stresses speed of response: actuar de inmediato, responder de inmediato.
reprimir una exclamaciónReprimir collocates with involuntary reactions one struggles to suppress: reprimir una sonrisa, reprimir las lágrimas, reprimir un bostezo. A go-to literary verb for bottled-up responses.
bailotearonDiminutive/frequentative of bailar: the -otear suffix suggests small, jerky, involuntary movement. Compare corretear (to scurry), parlotear (to chatter). The suffix makes the verb vivid and slightly comic.
estupefactoDumbstruck, stupefied — literary adjective. Quedarse estupefacto = to be left speechless with shock. Compare pasmado (dazed), atónito (astonished).
gramática
  • se inundó de una dura luz — Reflexive construction where the room is the subject 'flooding itself.' The agent (light) is expressed with de. Common pattern: se llenó de gente (it filled with people), se cubrió de nieve (it covered with snow).
  • asirse al borde — Asirse a = to grip, seize hold of. Literary/formal; everyday equivalent: agarrarse a. The reflexive a marks what is grabbed.

vocabulario

exánimeLifeless, drained of animation — literary word (lit. 'without anima', soul/breath). Used of bodies or faces that appear dead. More dramatic than inanimado, which applies to objects.
gramática

09 💬 Vampiro
Pero, entonces, el vampiro sonrió, casi anhelante, y la sustancia blanca y tersa de su rostro se movió con las líneas infinitamente flexibles pero mínimas de los dibujos animados. —¿Ves? —preguntó en voz queda. El muchacho tembló y levantó una mano como para defenderse de una luz demasiado poderosa.
But then the vampire smiled, almost longingly, and the white, smooth substance of his face moved with the infinitely flexible but spare lines of a cartoon. "You see?" he asked in a hushed voice. The boy shuddered and raised a hand as if to shield himself from a light too powerful to bear.
vocabulario
anhelanteLonging, yearning, wistful — from anhelar = to yearn for. Anhelante suggests barely-suppressed desire with a tinge of melancholy. Ansioso is closer to anxious/eager; anhelante is more poetic.
en voz quedaIn a hushed voice — quedo/a = still, soft, quiet. A literary set phrase; everyday equivalent: en voz baja. Quedo also appears in quedarse quedo (to go very still).
como para defenderseComo para + infinitive = as if to, as though about to. Concise way to imply purpose or half-formed intention: se inclinó como para escuchar (he leaned in as if to listen).

§ La entrevista comienza
12 💬 Vampiro / Muchacho / Vampiro
—Bien, ¿aún me quieres entrevistar? —preguntó el vampiro. El muchacho abrió la boca antes de poder contestar. Movió afirmativamente la cabeza. —Sí —dijo por fin. El vampiro tomó asiento lentamente frente a él e, inclinándose, le dijo cortés, confidencialmente: —No tengas miedo. Simplemente haz funcionar las cintas.
"Well, do you still want to interview me?" asked the vampire. The boy's mouth opened before he could answer. He nodded. "Yes," he finally said. The vampire sat down slowly across from him and, leaning in, said gently, confidentially: "Don't be afraid. Just start the tape."
vocabulario
por finFinally, at last — carries a note of relief or resolution after waiting. More emotionally loaded than neutral finalmente. Al fin is nearly identical but sounds slightly more literary.
tomó asientoTook a seat — more formal than se sentó. A set phrase used in formal narration or polite contexts: tome asiento, por favor.
confidencialmenteIn a confiding way — the adverb captures hushed disclosure. In everyday speech, en confianza is more colloquial for the same tone.
gramática
  • No tengas miedo — Tener miedo = to be afraid (lit. 'to have fear'). Spanish uses tener + noun where English uses adjectives: tener hambre (be hungry), tener razón (be right), tener sueño (be sleepy).
  • haz funcionar las cintas — Causative hacer + infinitive: to make something work/run. A productive construction: hacer esperar (to keep waiting), hacer saber (to let know), hacer entrar (to bring in).

vocabulario

cómodamenteComfortably, with composure — sentarse cómodamente often implies settling in unhurried, or — as here — a composed, deliberate posture.
gramática
  • más importante de lo que te puedes imaginar — When the second term of a comparison is a clause (not a noun), Spanish uses de lo que instead of que: es más difícil de lo que parece (it's harder than it looks).
  • Créeme — Imperative with enclitic pronoun: pronoun attaches to the end of affirmative imperatives. Negative flips it: no me creas.

§ El año 1791 — la tragedia
15 💬 Muchacho / Vampiro
—Usted no siempre fue un vampiro, ¿verdad? —preguntó. —No —contestó el vampiro—, era un hombre de veinticinco años cuando me convertí en un vampiro, y eso sucedió en mil setecientos noventa y uno.
"You weren't always a vampire, were you?" he began. "No," answered the vampire. "I was a twenty-five-year-old man when I became a vampire, and that was in seventeen ninety-one."
vocabulario
¿verdad?The all-purpose tag question — unlike English tags, it doesn't change form. ¿No? is more colloquial; ¿no es así? more formal. ¿Verdad? is neutral and works in any register.
gramática
  • cuando me convertí en vampiro — Convertirse en + noun: to become, to turn into (through transformation). Distinct from hacerse (become through effort: se hizo médico) and volverse (become through character change: se volvió cruel).

No creo que me gustara dar una respuesta tan fácil —dijo el vampiro—. Prefiero contar la historia verdadera... —Sí —dijo rápidamente el muchacho. Se pasaba una y otra vez el pañuelo por los labios.
The boy was struck by the precision of the date and repeated it before asking: "And how did that come about?" "There is a simple answer. I don't think I'd like to give one," said the vampire. vocabulario
quedó perplejoQuedar + adjective as a resultative: describes the state someone is left in after an event. Very common in narrative: quedó atónito (was left astonished), quedó sin palabras (was left speechless).

gramática
  • ¿Y eso cómo sucedió? — Topic fronting: the topic (eso) comes before the question word. Very natural colloquially: ¿Y tú cómo te llamas? The verb follows the question word, then the subject.
  • No creo que me gustara — Negated creer que triggers subjunctive. Affirmative: creo que me gustaría (indicative). Negated: no creo que me gustara (subjunctive). A reliable rule.

aclararse la gargantaTo clear one's throat — aclarar = to clear, clarify. The reflexive dative (se) marks it as his own throat. A fixed collocation.

gramática
  • sacudió la cabeza — The definite article replaces the possessive with body parts when the owner is clear from context: levantó la mano, cerró los ojos, sacudió la cabeza. Using su cabeza would sound unnatural.

vocabulario
echar una mirada vagaEchar una mirada = to cast a glance, give a look. Vaga = blank, unfocused, vacant. Echar as light verb: echar un vistazo (glance), echar una ojeada (quick look), echar un ojo (keep an eye on).
ganarse la vidaTo earn one's living, make a living — extremely common collocation. ¿A qué te dedicas? is the direct equivalent for 'what do you do for work.' Ganarse (reflexive) emphasizes the effort of earning.
por el momentoFor a moment (briefly) — can also mean 'for the time being' depending on context. Here it's purely temporal.
gramática
  • habíamos recibido — Pluperfect: marks an action completed before the narrative past moment. Spanish uses the pluperfect more strictly than English for sequencing events.
  • empezó a reírse — Empezar a + infinitive: ingressive aspect, marking the start of an action. Compare the aspect trilogy: empezar a (begin), seguir + gerund (continue), dejar de (stop).

gramática
  • de lo que jamás hubiéramos podido vivir — Pluperfect subjunctive in a comparative clause: after mejor de lo que, when the comparison is counterfactual, the pluperfect subjunctive is required. Hubiéramos podido = we could have (but never did/could).

Y aún puedo recordar esa música tenue y rápida y la visión del pantano que se elevaba más allá de ella, los cipreses cubiertos de musgo flotando contra el cielo. Y había los sonidos del pantano, un coro de criaturas, el grito de los pájaros. Creo que nos encantaba. Hacía que los muebles de palo de rosa parecieran más valiosos, la música, más delicada y deseable.
And I can still remember that thin, quick music and the vision of the swamp rising beyond her, the moss-hung cypresses floating against the sky. And there were the sounds of the swamp — a chorus of creatures, the cry of birds. I think we adored it. It made the rosewood furniture seem all the more precious, the music more delicate and desirable.
vocabulario
tenueFaint, delicate, barely-there — of sound: barely audible; of light: soft and dim; of fabric: fine and sheer. More evocative than débil (weak) or suave (soft) because it implies presence on the edge of perception.
encantabaEncantar = to love, adore, be enchanted by — much stronger than gustar. Like gustar, the subject is the thing loved, not the person: nos encantaba = we adored it.
gramática
  • Hacía que los muebles parecieran más valiosos — Hacer que + subjunctive (causative with different subjects). When the caused action has its own subject, use hacer que + subjunctive: hizo que ella saliera. Same subject: hacer + bare infinitive: se hizo esperar.

Aun cuando la glicina arrancaba las contraventanas de las ventanas del ático y enredaba sus zarcillos en la pared de cal en menos de un año...
Even as the wisteria tore the shutters from the attic windows and worked its tendrils into the whitewashed brick in under a year...
vocabulario
aun cuandoEven when / even though — concessive conjunction. Aun cuando + indicative = even when (factual). Aun cuando + subjunctive = even if (hypothetical). The distinction maps onto the indicative/subjunctive contrast for real vs. imagined.
arrancarTo tear out/off, rip away, pull up by the roots — implies force and often irreversibility. Versatile: arrancar una página (tear out a page), arrancar el motor (start/rev the engine), arrancar un árbol (uproot a tree).
enredar sus zarcillosEnredar = to tangle, wind, entangle. Zarcillo = tendril. Enredarse en = to get tangled in (also figuratively: enredarse en un asunto = to get mixed up in something).

... Sí, nos encantaba. A todos menos a mi hermano. Creo que nunca lo oí quejarse de algo, pero yo sabía cómo se sentía. Mi padre había muerto ya, y yo era el jefe de familia y tenía que defenderlo constantemente de mi madre y hermana. Querían llevarlo de visita y a Nueva Orleans para fiestas, pero él odiaba esas cosas.
"...Yes, we adored it. All of us except my brother. I don't think I ever heard him complain about anything, but I knew how he felt. My father was already dead, and I was head of the family, and I had to defend him constantly from my mother and sister. They wanted to take him visiting and to New Orleans for parties, but he hated those things."
vocabulario
a todos menos aAll except — menos as an exclusion marker. Todos menos él = everyone except him. Colloquial and concise; more formal options: excepto, salvo.
ir de visitaTo go visiting, pay calls — de + noun forms manner/activity phrases: ir de compras (go shopping), ir de paseo (go for a walk), estar de viaje (be traveling). The noun names the activity, not the destination.

Creo que dejó de ir del todo antes de cumplir los doce años: La oración era lo que le importaba, la oración y las vidas de los santos encuadernadas en cuero.
I think he stopped going altogether before he turned twelve. Prayer was what mattered to him — prayer and his leather-bound lives of the saints.
vocabulario
del todoEntirely, completely, altogether — no del todo = not entirely. Intensifier of completion: desapareció del todo (vanished completely), no lo entiendo del todo (I don't quite follow it).
cumplir los doce añosCumplir años = to turn (an age), to have a birthday — specifically implies completing a full year. ¿Cuándo cumples? = When's your birthday? Not the same as tener (which states current age).
importarle algo a alguienImportar = to matter (like gustar, takes an indirect object). Lo que le importaba = what mattered to him. ¿Qué te importa? can mean 'What does it matter to you?' or, rudely, 'Mind your own business.'
encuadernadas en cueroEncuadernar = to bind a book. Encuadernado en cuero/tela/rústica = leather-/cloth-/paperback-bound. A bookbinding collocation — the root isn't obvious from the meaning.
