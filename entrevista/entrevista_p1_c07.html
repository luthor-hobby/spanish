Primera parte — Pasaje 7

Louis es ya vampiro completo, atado a Lestat por necesidad. Lestat le enseña las lecciones prácticas — viajar en barco, negocios nocturnos. Pero Louis ve en Lestat un hombre incapaz de maravillarse. Describe Nueva Orleans tal como era: una ciudad de franceses, españoles, africanos, indios y americanos, mágica y cosmopolita. Los Freniere: cinco hermanas y un joven hermano que Lestat quiere matar. Louis siente por las hermanas y protege al joven Freniere. En el duelo, Lestat aprovecha el momento para matarlo.

§ Con el maestro que odio — las lecciones prácticas
01 💬 Vampiro / Muchacho / Vampiro
subjuntivo
—Ya ves —el vampiro se dirigió al muchacho—. Por primera vez yo era completa y cabalmente un vampiro. Cerré las persianas y me encerré en mi ataúd. Así es como me convertí en vampiro. —Y allí estabas —dijo el muchacho después de una pausa—, junto a otro vampiro al que odiabas. —... Pero tenía que quedarme a su lado —le contestó el vampiro—. Como ya te he dicho, me tenía en una gran desventaja.
"You see," the vampire said to the boy. "For the first time I was completely and utterly a vampire. I closed the blinds and locked myself in my coffin. That is how I became a vampire." "And there you were," the boy said after a pause, "alongside another vampire you hated." "... But I had to stay by his side," the vampire answered him. "As I've already told you, he had me at a great disadvantage."
vocabulario
encerré enlocked myself in; confined myself to — reflexive verb expressing seclusion
quedarme a su ladoto stay/remain by someone's side — collocation expressing obligation/dependency
cabalmentecompletely, absolutely; thoroughly and utterly
gramática
  • cerré las persianas y me encerré — preterite: two sequential actions. The reflexive encerré emphasizes voluntary confinement.
  • tenía que quedarme — imperfect of necessity: expresses a continuous obligation in the past.
02
Insinuaba que había mucho que yo no sabía y debía saber y que sólo él podía decirme. Pero de hecho, la parte principal de lo que me enseñó era práctica.
He insinuated that there was much I did not know and ought to know, and that only he could tell me. But in fact, the main part of what he taught me was practical.
vocabulario

debía saberought to know; should know — moral/intellectual obligation
gramática
  • había mucho que yo no sabía — subjunctive construction: noun clause with 'mucho' + relative clause. Debía saber implies obligation implied by insinuation.
03
Cómo podríamos viajar, por ejemplo, en barco, haciendo que nuestros ataúdes fueran transportados como si contuvieran los restos de seres queridos que se mandaban aquí o allá para ser enterrados; cómo nadie se atrevería a abrir semejante ataúd, y podríamos levantarnos de él de noche para limpiar el barco de ratas.
How we could travel, for example, by ship, by having our coffins transported as if they contained the remains of beloved ones sent here or there to be buried; how no one would dare open such a coffin, and how we could rise from it at night to clean the ship of rats.
vocabulario

levantarnos de él de nocheto rise from it at night — rise up, emerge from
gramática
  • haciendo que... fueran transportados — causative construction: hacer + que + subjunctive, expressing causing/making something happen.
  • como si contuvieran — imperfect subjunctive: counterfactual condition (as if they contained).
04

Y en todos estos asuntos mundanos, Lestat era un maestro adecuado.
And then there were the shops he knew, whose owners would receive us well after closing hour to outfit us with the finest fashions from Paris, and those agents who were willing to conduct financial matters in restaurants and cabarets. And in all these worldly affairs, Lestat was an adequate master.
vocabulario

gramática
  • cuyos dueños nos recibían bien — relative clause with possessive pronoun: cuyo agrees with dueños (masculine plural).
05 💬 Lestat
subjuntivo
—¿No te cuido acaso con un esplendor de príncipes? —le gritaba Lestat—. ¿No te doy todos los lujos que jamás habrías podido imaginar? ¡Deja de lloriquear con ese disparate tuyo sobre las viejas amistades! ¡Tus viejos amigos están muertos! ¿Por qué no te mueres y me dejas en paz con mi dinero? El anciano sollozaba y decía que esas cosas poco le importaban en la vejez.
"Do I not care for you with princely splendor?" Lestat shouted at him. "Do I not give you every luxury you could never have imagined?" Stop whining about that nonsense of yours regarding old friendships! Your old friends are dead! Why don't you die and leave me in peace with my money?" The old man would sob and say that such things mattered little to him in his old age.

06
Se hubiera quedado contento en su pequeña granja para siempre. Yo quería preguntarle a menudo: ¿Dónde estaba esa granja? ¿De dónde viniste a Luisiana? Para obtener alguna pista sobre el lugar donde Lestat podría haber conocido a otro vampiro. Pero no me atrevía a sacar esos temas, no fuera que el anciano se pusiera a llorar y Lestat se enfureciera.
He would have been happy to stay on his little farm forever. I wanted to ask him often: Where was that farm? Where did you come to Louisiana from? To get some clue about the place where Lestat might have met another vampire. But I did not dare bring up those subjects, lest the old man start to cry and Lestat become enraged.
vocabulario

gramática
  • se hubiera quedado contento — pluperfect subjunctive (conditional structure): hypothetical condition without an if-clause.
  • no fuera que el anciano se pusiera — subjunctive in negative purpose clause: fear/prevention of action.

§ Nueva Orleans — la ciudad mágica
08 💬 Vampiro
subjuntivo
—La existencia, como he dicho, era posible. Siempre había la promesa detrás de su burlona sonrisa de que sabía grandes cosas o cosas terribles. Y todo el tiempo me menospreciaba y me atacaba por mi amor a los sentidos, mi renuencia a matar, y el casi desmayo que matar podía producirme.
"Existence, as I've said, was possible. There was always the promise behind his mocking smile that he knew great things or terrible things. And all the while he scorned me and attacked me for my love of sensation, my reluctance to kill, and the near-fainting that killing could produce in me.
vocabulario

09
Pero lo que sucedió fue simplemente que, en mi disgusto por él, llegué a ignorarlo y a sospecharlo, y sin embargo a estudiarlo con una fascinación distante. —Permíteme que te describa Nueva Orleans, como era entonces, para que puedas comprender qué sencillas eran nuestras vidas. No había ciudad en América como Nueva Orleans.
But what happened was simply that, in my disgust with him, I came to ignore him and to suspect him, and yet to study him with a distant fascination. Allow me to describe New Orleans to you, as it was then, so that you can understand how simple our lives were. There was no city in America like New Orleans.
vocabulario

gramática
  • llegué a ignorarlo y a sospecharlo — parallel infinitives: repeating a + infinitive for emphasis and rhythm.
  • para que puedas comprender — subjunctive in purpose clause: so that you may understand.

It was full not only of French and Spanish of all classes who had in part formed its peculiar aristocracy, but also of immigrants of every kind, the Irish and Germans in particular.

And then there were not only black slaves, but the great growing class of free persons of color, that wonderful people of mixed blood, who produced a magnificent and unique caste of artisans, artists, poets, and acknowledged female beauty.

And above all, gliding through this medley of tongues and colors, were the sailors from the ships, who arrived in great waves to spend their money, to purchase beautiful women by night, to dine on the best of Spanish and French cooking and drink wines imported from around the world.

13 💬 Vampiro
Así era Nueva Orleans: un lugar magnífico y mágico para vivir. Un lugar en el cual un vampiro, ricamente vestido y caminando con gracia por los charcos de luz de una farola de gas tras otra, podría no atraer más atención en la noche que cientos de otras criaturas exóticas, si atraía alguna.
So it was New Orleans: a magnificent and magical place to live. A place in which a vampire, richly dressed and walking with grace through pools of light from one gas lamp to another, could attract no more attention in the night than hundreds of other exotic creatures, if it attracted any.

14

15

El monumento no dice que tal o cual hombre caminó aquí. No, dice que lo que él sintió en un tiempo en un lugar continúa. Mientras permanezcan los monumentos, continúa.
It is extraordinary that many of those men and women who stayed for whatever reason left behind them some monument, some structure of marble and brick and stone that still stands. The monument does not say that such and such a man walked here. No, it says that what he felt at one time in one place continues. As long as the monuments remain, it continues.

§ Los Freniere — el duelo
17 💬 Vampiro
—De qué hablaba. Sí, dinero. Lestat y yo teníamos que ganar dinero. Pero deja que te dé un ejemplo perfecto de lo que le gustaba a Lestat. Al norte, por el río, estaba la plantación Freniere, una magnífica extensión de tierras. Vivían allí cinco jóvenes mujeres y un joven.
"What I was talking about. Yes, money. Lestat and I had to make money. But let me give you a perfect example of what Lestat enjoyed. To the north, along the river, was the Freniere plantation, a magnificent expanse of land. There lived five young women and one young man.

He was the one who had to manage the plantation; the one who had to negotiate marriages, gather dowries when the entire fortune of the place hung precariously on the next year's harvest; he was the one who had to negotiate, struggle, and keep the entire material world at bay for the world of Freniere.
