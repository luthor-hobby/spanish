Entrevista con el Vampiro

Anne Rice · Capítulo 6: Primera parte

Pasaje 6

Primera parte — Pasaje 6

Louis reflexiona sobre la diferencia entre él y Lestat. Mata al primer humano: la experiencia lo abruma. Lestat lo golpea cuando se aferra al cadáver. A medianoche en la galería, Louis decide vivir con reverencia. El muchacho lo compara con el amor. Lestat hace el truco de la rata — llenando una copa de cristal con sangre de rata. Descubren que pueden vivir de animales. Louis amenaza con echar a Lestat de la plantación. Louis duerme solo en el oratorio de su hermano. Por primera vez es vampiro de verdad.

§ La primera matanza — el estremecimiento
01 💬 Lestat
subjuntivo
—Me enfermas —me dijo cuando regresó a mi lado. Era como si fuésemos insectos negros totalmente camuflados en la noche, observando a los esclavos que se movían, ajenos a nosotros, descubriendo al hombre herido, arrastrándolo de vuelta, abriéndose en abanico por el follaje en busca del agresor. —Vamos, tenemos que capturar otro antes de que regresen al campamento.
"You make me sick," he said to me when he returned to my side. It was as if we were black insects totally camouflaged in the night, watching the slaves who moved about, unaware of us, discovering the wounded man, dragging him back, fanning out through the foliage in search of the attacker. "Come on, we have to capture another before they return to camp."
vocabulario
enfermar a alguiento make someone sick — me enfermas: you disgust me, you make me ill; can be literal or (as here) expressing contempt/disgust
ajenos a nosotrosunaware of us, oblivious to us — ajeno a = foreign to, unaware of; ajeno can mean 'belonging to another' or 'oblivious'
abrirse en abanicoto fan out — abanico = a fan; abrirse en abanico: to spread out in a fan shape; used for people dispersing to search an area
en busca dein search of — a fixed prepositional phrase: salir en busca de, ir en busca de
arrastrar de vueltato drag back — arrastrar = to drag; de vuelta = back (to the starting point)
gramática
  • como si fuésemos insectos — Como si + imperfecto de subjuntivo: fuésemos is the imperfect subjunctive of ser (alternate form of fuéramos). Both forms are correct; -se forms are more common in literary/formal Spanish.
  • antes de que regresen al campamento — Antes de que + subjuntivo: 'before they return.' Antes de que always takes the subjunctive regardless of tense.
02
—Y rápidamente fuimos en busca de uno que se había separado de los otros. Yo estaba muy agitado, convencido de que no podría obligarme a atacar y sin sentir ningún deseo de hacerlo. Había muchas cosas que Lestat podría haber dicho o hecho. Podría haber hecho rica la experiencia en tantas maneras. Pero no lo hizo.
And quickly we went in search of one who had separated from the others. I was very agitated, convinced I couldn't force myself to attack, and feeling no desire to do so. There were many things Lestat could have said or done. He could have enriched the experience in so many ways. But he didn't.
vocabulario
en busca dein search of — see previous sentence; repeated here to show Louis and Lestat now hunting
separarse de los otrosto separate from the others, to stray from the group — separarse de = to move away from; used for straying from a herd, group, or path
obligarse ato force oneself to — obligar = to force/compel; reflexive obligarse a = to make oneself do something
hacer rica la experienciato enrich the experience — hacer + adjective + noun: causative; to make the experience rich/meaningful
gramática
  • podría haber dicho o hecho... Podría haber hecho rica — Condicional compuesto (irrealidad en el pasado): 'could have said/done.' Lestat had the capacity but didn't use it — an unrealized past possibility.
  • Pero no lo hizo. — Proforma verbal: 'But he didn't.' No lo hizo = he did not do it; lo stands in for the enriching actions just described.
03 💬 Vampiro / Lestat
—Matar no es una acción común —dijo el vampiro—. Uno no se sacia simplemente con sangre. Es la experiencia de la vida de otro con certeza, y a menudo la experiencia de la pérdida de esa vida a través de la sangre. Es nuevamente y nuevamente la celebración de esa experiencia; porque para los vampiros ésa es la experiencia última. No creo que Lestat apreciara nunca eso. —Hazlo —dijo—.
"Killing is not a common act," said the vampire. "One is not simply satiated with blood. It is the experience of another's life with certainty, and often the experience of the loss of that life through the blood. It is again and again the celebration of that experience; because for vampires that is the ultimate experience. I don't think Lestat ever appreciated that." "Do it," he said.
vocabulario
saciarse conto be satiated with, to have one's fill of — saciar = to satisfy fully; no se sacia simplemente con sangre: blood alone is not enough
con certezawith certainty, for certain — certeza = certainty; con certeza as an adverbial phrase meaning 'definitively'
nuevamente y nuevamenteagain and again — emphatic repetition; more literary than una y otra vez
la experiencia últimathe ultimate experience — último here = ultimate/final in a superlative sense, not 'last in sequence'
gramática
  • No creo que Lestat apreciara nunca eso — No creer que + subjuntivo: 'I don't think Lestat ever appreciated that.' No creer que triggers the subjunctive because it negates a belief. Apreciara is the imperfect subjunctive, used here for a general past state.
04
Ahora no puedes echarte atrás. Abrumado por la repulsión y débil de frustración, obedecí. Me arrodillé al lado del hombre agachado, que trataba de resistir, y, aferrándome a sus hombros con ambas manos, me clavé en su cuello. Mis dientes apenas habían empezado a cambiar, y tuve que desgarrar la carne, no perforarla; pero una vez que se hizo la herida, la sangre fluyó.
"You can't back out now." Overwhelmed by revulsion and weak with frustration, I obeyed. I knelt beside the crouching man, who was trying to resist, and, gripping his shoulders with both hands, I sank into his neck. My teeth had barely begun to change, and I had to tear the flesh, not pierce it; but once the wound was made, the blood flowed.
vocabulario
echarse atrásto back out, to back down — echarse atrás: to withdraw, to renege on a commitment; an idiomatic phrasal verb
abrumado poroverwhelmed by — abrumar = to overwhelm; past participle used as adjective; common with emotions: abrumado por la culpa, el dolor
débil de frustraciónweak with frustration — débil de + emotion: 'weak from'; the emotion is expressed as the source of physical weakness
aferrarse ato grip, to cling to — aferrarse a: to seize and hold tight; used literally and figuratively
desgarrar la carneto tear the flesh — desgarrar = to tear, to rip apart; stronger and more violent than rasgar
una vez que se hizo la heridaonce the wound was made — una vez que + preterite: 'once X happened'
gramática
  • aferrándome a sus hombros... me clavé en su cuello — Gerundio de simultaneidad: aferrándome describes the simultaneous gripping action while the main verb me clavé carries the narrative forward.
  • tuve que desgarrar la carne, no perforarla — Tener que + infinitivo: concrete necessity. The negated infinitive no perforarla is a sharp contrast — his teeth aren't yet properly formed.
05
Y una vez que sucedió aquello, una vez que me aferré a ella, bebiendo... todo lo demás desapareció.
And once that happened, once I clung to it, drinking... everything else disappeared.
vocabulario
una vez queonce — temporal connector; takes indicative in past narration: una vez que sucedió = once it happened
todo lo demáseverything else — lo demás = the rest, everything else; a neuter construction
desaparecerto disappear — the moment Louis starts drinking, consciousness of everything outside narrows to the act itself
gramática
  • una vez que sucedió aquello, una vez que me aferré... — Repetición anafórica: the double una vez que creates a rhythmic build-up before the climax ('everything else disappeared'). The repetition mimics the moment of surrender.
06 💬 Lestat
—¡Está muerto, imbécil! —dijo con su encanto y tacto característicos—. ¡No puedes beber cuando ya están muertos! ¡Entiéndelo! Me puse frenético un instante, fuera de mí, e insistí en que el corazón del hombre aún latía, y estaba en agonía por aferrarme a él de nuevo. Le corrí las manos por el pecho, luego le cogí las muñecas. —Morirás si haces eso —dijo Lestat—.
"He's dead, you idiot!" he said with his characteristic charm and tact. "You can't drink when they're already dead! Understand it!" I went frantic for a moment, beside myself, and insisted that the man's heart was still beating, and I was in agony to cling to him again. I ran my hands over his chest, then grabbed his wrists. "You'll die if you do that," said Lestat.
vocabulario
con su encanto y tacto característicoswith his characteristic charm and tact — heavy irony; Lestat has neither
ponerse frenéticoto go frantic, to become frenzied — ponerse + adjective: onset of state; frenético = frantic, frenzied
fuera de síbeside oneself, out of one's mind — a fixed idiom: estar fuera de sí = to be beside oneself with emotion
correr las manos porto run one's hands over — correr las manos por: to pass/sweep one's hands along a surface; a tactile searching gesture
estar en agonía porto be in agony to — agonía = agony, but here used for intense longing/craving, not just pain
gramática
  • insistí en que el corazón aún latía — Insistir en que + indicativo: 'insisted that.' When insistir en que reports a claimed fact, it takes the indicative (he asserted it was true). If expressing a demand, it would take the subjunctive.
07
Te llevará a la muerte si te aferras a él en la muerte. Cuando nos sentamos en la sala, Lestat se puso a jugar un solitario sobre la madera pulida de la mesa. Vi mi vida como si estuviera aparte de ella. Por la mañana, me di cuenta de que era su completo superior y que había sido miserablemente burlado al tenerlo como maestro.
"It will lead you to death if you cling to him in death." When we sat in the parlor, Lestat began to play solitaire on the polished wood of the table. I saw my life as if I stood apart from it. By morning, I realized that I was his complete superior and that I had been miserably deceived in having him as a teacher.
vocabulario
llevar a la muerteto lead to death — llevar a + destination/outcome: to lead to, to bring to; llevarle a = to take someone to
ponerse a jugar un solitarioto begin playing solitaire — ponerse a + infinitive: sudden onset; solitario = solitaire (card game)
madera pulidapolished wood — pulido = polished, refined; from pulir
ser el completo superior deto be someone's complete superior — an unusual superlative construction; Louis measuring himself against Lestat
burladodeceived, cheated, mocked — burlar = to deceive, to mock; ser burlado = to be duped
gramática
  • me di cuenta de que era su completo superior y que había sido burlado — Darse cuenta de que + indicativo: 'I realized that.' Darse cuenta de que always takes the indicative because it reports a perceived reality.
  • al tenerlo como maestro — Al + infinitivo: 'in having him as a teacher.' Here al + infinitive expresses circumstance or condition — the means by which he was deceived.
08
Debía guiarme a través de las lecciones necesarias, y yo debía tolerar en él un estado mental que era una blasfemia contra la vida misma.
He was supposed to guide me through the necessary lessons, and I was supposed to tolerate in him a state of mind that was a blasphemy against life itself.
vocabulario
guiar a través deto guide through — guiar a través de: to lead someone through a process or experience
tolerar en alguiento tolerate in someone — tolerar en él: to put up with a quality that exists in him
una blasfemia contraa blasphemy against — blasfemia contra: an offense/affront to; used both literally (religion) and figuratively
la vida mismalife itself — mismo/a as a postpositive intensifier: la vida misma, el hombre mismo, yo mismo
gramática
  • debía guiarme... yo debía tolerar — Deber en imperfecto = obligación frustrada o expectativa: 'was supposed to.' The imperfect of deber conveys an obligation that existed but may not have been fulfilled — here, the implicit contract Lestat violated.
§ La galería a medianoche — como el amor
09 💬 Vampiro
Bien pasada la medianoche, me puse por último de pie y salí a la galería. La luna era grande sobre los cipreses, y la luz de las velas se derramaba por las puertas abiertas. Los gruesos pilares y paredes de yeso de la casa habían sido encalados recientemente, y una lluvia de verano había dejado la noche limpia y chispeante de gotas de agua.
Well past midnight, I finally rose to my feet and went out to the gallery. The moon was large over the cypresses, and the candlelight spilled through the open doors. The thick pillars and plaster walls of the house had been recently whitewashed, and a summer rain had left the night clean and sparkling with drops of water.
vocabulario
bien pasada la medianochewell past midnight — bien as an intensifier before a past participle: bien pasada = well past, long after; also bien entrado el siglo
ponerse de pieto rise to one's feet, to stand up — ponerse de pie: the standard expression for standing up from a seated/lying position
derramarse porto spill through, to pour through — derramarse = to spill, to overflow; used for light, liquid, sound spreading through a space
encaladowhitewashed — encalar = to whitewash; lime-washed walls typical of colonial Louisiana architecture
chispeante de gotas de aguasparkling with drops of water — chispeante = sparkling, glittering; from chispa (spark)
gramática
  • habían sido encalados recientemente — Pluscuamperfecto pasivo: 'had been recently whitewashed.' The compound passive (haber sido + past participle) situates the whitewashing before the night being described.
10
Me apoyé en el último pilar de la galería, con la cabeza tocando los suaves zarcillos de un jazmín que crecía allí, y pensé en lo que yacía ante mí a través del mundo y a través del tiempo, y resolví abordar todo ello con delicadeza y reverencia, aprendiendo de cada cosa lo que mejor me llevaría a la siguiente.
I leaned against the last pillar of the gallery, my head touching the soft tendrils of a jasmine that grew there, and I thought about what lay before me across the world and across time, and I resolved to approach all of it with delicacy and reverence, learning from each thing what would best lead me to the next.
vocabulario
apoyarse ento lean against — apoyarse en = to rest against/on; also to rely on: apoyarse en un amigo
zarcillos de jazmínjasmine tendrils — zarcillo = a tendril (of a plant) or an earring; here the soft curling shoots of jasmine
yacer ante míto lie before me — yacer: literary verb 'to lie'; lo que yacía ante mí = what lay ahead of him in time and space
resolver + infinitivoto resolve to — resolví abordar: I resolved to approach; resolver = to resolve, to decide firmly
abordar con delicadezato approach with delicacy — abordar = to approach, to tackle; con delicadeza = gently, with care
gramática
  • aprendiendo de cada cosa lo que mejor me llevaría a la siguiente — Gerundio de modo + relativa: the gerund (aprendiendo) describes the manner of Louis's resolve. Inside it, lo que mejor me llevaría = 'what would best lead me' — a relative clause with conditional force.
11
¿Comprendes lo que quiero decir cuando digo que no deseaba lanzarme de cabeza a la experiencia, que lo que había sentido como vampiro era demasiado poderoso para desperdiciarlo?
Do you understand what I mean when I say I did not want to throw myself headlong into experience, that what I had felt as a vampire was too powerful to waste?
vocabulario
lanzarse de cabeza ato throw oneself headlong into — lanzarse de cabeza: to dive in head-first; implies reckless, unthinking immersion
demasiado... paratoo... to — demasiado poderoso para desperdiciarlo: too powerful to waste; the standard structure for 'too X to do Y'
desperdiciarto waste — desperdiciar: to throw away, to squander; used for wasting time, talent, opportunity, experience
gramática
  • lo que había sentido como vampiro era demasiado poderoso para desperdiciarlo — Demasiado + adjetivo + para + infinitivo: 'too powerful to waste it.' The -lo on the infinitive refers back to lo que había sentido — the experience itself.
12 💬 Muchacho / Vampiro
subjuntivo
—Sí —dijo deprisa el joven—. Parece como si hubiera estado enamorado. Al vampiro le brillaron los ojos. —Exacto. Es como el amor —sonrió—. Y deja que te cuente mis pensamientos de esa noche para que puedas saber que hay profundas diferencias entre los vampiros, y cómo llegué a adoptar un enfoque diferente al de Lestat. Lestat nunca fue el vampiro que soy yo. Para nada.
"Yes," the young man said quickly. "It sounds as if you had been in love." The vampire's eyes brightened. "Exactly. It's like love," he smiled. "And let me tell you my thoughts of that night so that you can know that there are profound differences among vampires, and how I came to adopt a different approach from Lestat's. Lestat was never the vampire that I am. Not at all."
vocabulario
deprisaquickly, fast — an adverb; also written de prisa; synonym of rápidamente; used here to show the boy's excited response
le brillaron los ojoshis eyes brightened/lit up — involuntary bodily reaction with dative: brillarle los ojos a alguien = for someone's eyes to light up
deja que te cuentelet me tell you — dejar que + subjunctive: 'let (someone) do'; deja que te cuente = allow me to tell you
adoptar un enfoqueto adopt an approach — enfoque = approach, focus, perspective; adoptar un enfoque diferente: to take a different tack
para nadanot at all — emphatic negation standing alone as a sentence; see also pasaje 3
gramática
  • parece como si hubiera estado enamorado — Parecer como si + pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo: 'it sounds as if you had been in love.' The pluperfect marks the love as something that preceded and explains the current telling.
  • para que puedas saber — Para que + subjuntivo (finalidad): 'so that you can know.' Purpose clauses always take the subjunctive.
13
—Entonces hizo algo que me iba a mostrar un camino para aprender.
Then he did something that was going to show me a way to learn.
vocabulario
ir a + infinitivo (en pasado)was going to — iba a mostrar: the imperfect of ir a = was going to; marks a future-in-the-past, building narrative suspense
un camino paraa way to, a path for — camino = road/path; used figuratively: un camino para aprender = a way of learning
gramática
  • que me iba a mostrar un camino — Ir a + infinitivo en imperfecto: future-in-the-past. The relative clause describes the significance of the action before the action itself is revealed — classic suspense-building in narration.
§ El truco de la rata — podemos vivir de animales
14 💬 Lestat
Tenía un respeto más que normal por las riquezas de Pointe du Lac. Y ahora tomó de uno de los armarios de porcelana una copa de cristal y dijo: —Me gustan los vasos. —Sólo que lo dijo con un deleite impío que me llevó a estudiarlo con ojos duros. ¡Lo detestaba intensamente! —Quiero enseñarte un pequeño truco —dijo.
He had more than a normal respect for the riches of Pointe du Lac. And now he took a crystal glass from one of the porcelain cabinets and said: "I like glasses." Except that he said it with an ungodly delight that led me to study him with hard eyes. I detested him intensely! "I want to show you a little trick," he said.
vocabulario
más que normalmore than normal, more than ordinary — a comparative collocation used to flag something as exceeding the expected degree
deleite impíoungodly delight — deleite = delight, pleasure; impío = ungodly, impious; the combination captures Lestat's gleeful irreverence
estudiar con ojos durosto study with hard eyes, to fix with a cold stare — ojos duros: eyes hardened by contempt; a loaded expression for hostile scrutiny
detestarto detest, to loathe — stronger than odiar (hate); implies active, visceral aversion
un pequeño trucoa little trick — truco = trick, knack; pequeño here signals Lestat's casual dismissal of what is actually a revelation
gramática
  • sólo que lo dijo con un deleite impío — Sólo que as a corrective: 'except that.' Sólo que introduces a qualification that undercuts what has just been said — he said the words, but the spirit behind them was different.
15
Y después de poner la copa en la mesa de juego, salió a la galería y, en un instante, había saltado la barandilla y caído suavemente en la tierra, y luego se lanzó hacia la oscuridad para coger algo con ambas manos. Cuando se puso ante mí con ello, me quedé atónito al ver que era una rata. —No seas tan maldito idiota —dijo—. ¿Acaso nunca has visto una rata?
And after placing the glass on the gaming table, he went out to the gallery and, in an instant, had leaped over the railing and landed softly on the ground, and then launched himself into the darkness to catch something with both hands. When he stood before me with it, I was astonished to see it was a rat. "Don't be such a damned idiot," he said. "Haven't you ever seen a rat?"
vocabulario
mesa de juegogaming table — a card table or game table; the same table Lestat was playing solitaire on
saltar la barandillato leap over the railing — saltar = to jump/leap; barandilla = railing, balustrade
lanzarse hacia la oscuridadto launch oneself into the darkness — lanzarse: to hurl oneself; marks Lestat's predatory speed
quedarse atónitoto be left astonished, to be stunned — atónito = astonished, dumbfounded; quedarse + adjective = resulting state
¿acaso nunca has visto?haven't you ever seen? — acaso in rhetorical questions implies the answer is obvious; see also pasaje 4
gramática
  • en un instante, había saltado la barandilla y caído suavemente — Pluscuamperfecto narrativo: the pluperfect here captures a rapid sequence of actions completed within the blink of an eye, before the main narrative resumes with se lanzó.
  • me quedé atónito al ver que era una rata — Al + infinitivo (causa): 'upon seeing that it was a rat.' Al ver = on seeing, upon realizing.
16
Era una rata inmensa y con una cola larga. La tenía del pescuezo para que no pudiera morderle. —Las ratas pueden ser bastante buenas —dijo. Y llevó la rata hasta la copa de vino, le cortó el cuello, y llenó rápidamente la copa con sangre. La rata fue luego volando por encima de la barandilla de la galería, y Lestat sostuvo la copa de vino a la vela con aire de triunfo.
It was an enormous rat with a long tail. He held it by the scruff of the neck so it couldn't bite him. "Rats can be quite good," he said. And he brought the rat to the wine glass, cut its throat, and quickly filled the glass with blood. The rat then went flying over the gallery railing, and Lestat held the wine glass up to the candle with an air of triumph.
vocabulario
tener del pescuezoto hold by the scruff of the neck — pescuezo = scruff/nape of the neck (of an animal); tener del pescuezo = to hold by the scruff
para que no pudiera morderleso it couldn't bite him — purpose clause with subjunctive; para que + subjunctive = so that
cortar el cuelloto cut the throat — cuello = neck/collar; cortar el cuello = to slit the throat
ir volandoto go flying — ir + gerund of motion verb; the rat is flung over the railing: it 'went flying'
con aire de triunfowith an air of triumph — aire de + noun: 'an air of'; a literary expression for a person's bearing or manner
gramática
  • La tenía del pescuezo para que no pudiera morderle — Para que + imperfecto de subjuntivo: purpose in a past narrative context. Pudiera = imperfect subjunctive of poder, expressing the feared potential action being prevented.
17
—Puede que tengas que vivir de ratas de vez en cuando, así que quítate esa expresión de la cara —dijo—. Ratas, pollos, ganado.
"You may have to live on rats from time to time, so wipe that expression off your face," he said. "Rats, chickens, cattle."
vocabulario
puede queit may be that, perhaps — puede que + subjunctive: expresses possibility; similar to quizá(s) or tal vez
vivir deto live on — vivir de + food/resource: to subsist on; vivir de ratas = to feed on rats
de vez en cuandofrom time to time, occasionally — a key fixed phrase for intermittent frequency
quitarse una expresión de la carato wipe an expression off one's face — quitarse de la cara: to remove from one's face; used colloquially for controlling one's expression
gramática
  • puede que tengas que vivir de ratas — Puede que + subjuntivo: 'it may be that you'll have to.' Puede que always takes the subjunctive. Note the double obligation: puede que (possibility) + tener que (necessity).
18 💬 Louis / Lestat / Louis / Lestat / Louis / Lestat
—¿Quieres decir que podemos vivir de los animales? —le pregunté. —Así es —dijo, y entonces arrojó la copa a la chimenea; yo miré los pedazos—. No te importa, ¿verdad? —Señaló el vaso roto con una sonrisa sarcástica. —Si me importa, te puedo sacar a ti y a tu padre de Pointe du Lac —dije; y creo que ésta fue mi primera muestra de temperamento. —¿Por qué habrías de hacerlo?
"Do you mean we can live on animals?" I asked him. "That's right," he said, and then threw the glass into the fireplace; I looked at the pieces. "You don't mind, do you?" He pointed to the broken glass with a sarcastic smile. "If I mind, I can put you and your father out of Pointe du Lac," I said; and I think this was my first show of temper. "Why would you do that?"
vocabulario
así esthat's right, that's how it is — a confirmation; así es = exactly so, that's correct
arrojarto throw, to hurl — arrojar is more forceful than tirar; suggests a deliberate, emphatic throw
los pedazosthe pieces, the shards — pedazo = piece, fragment; mirar los pedazos: the broken glass as a kind of punctuation
importarto mind, to matter — ¿no te importa? = you don't mind?; si me importa = if I care/mind
sacar a alguien deto put someone out of, to remove someone from — sacar de: to take out of, to remove from; here a threat of eviction
muestra de temperamentoshow of temper — muestra = sample, show, display; temperamento here = temper, spiritedness
gramática
  • ¿por qué habrías de hacerlo? — Condicional de obligación: haber de: 'why would you do that?' / 'why should you do that?' Haber de + infinitive expresses obligation or expectation; in the conditional, it asks what grounds someone has for an action.
19
—me preguntó con falsa alarma—. Aún no sabes todo... ¿o sí? —Reía entonces y caminaba lentamente por la habitación—. ¿Sabes, por ejemplo, todas las maneras en que puedes morir? Y morir ahora sería semejante calamidad, ¿no es así? —Debe de haber alguien en el mundo que me pueda enseñar esas cosas —dije—. Por cierto, ¡no eres el único vampiro! Y tu padre tiene quizá setenta años.
He asked me with feigned alarm. "You don't know everything yet... or do you?" He was laughing then and walking slowly around the room. "Do you know, for example, all the ways in which you can die? And dying now would be such a calamity, wouldn't it?" "There must be someone in the world who can teach me those things," I said. "Incidentally, you're not the only vampire! And your father is perhaps seventy years old."
vocabulario
con falsa alarmawith feigned alarm — falso = false, feigned; Lestat performing concern he doesn't feel

  • ¿sabes todas las maneras en que puedes morir? — Relativa especificativa con indicativo: 'all the ways in which you can die.' The relative clause specifies a real set of existing ways — indicative because they are factual, not hypothetical.
  • morir ahora sería semejante calamidad — Condicional de hipótesis: 'dying now would be such a calamity.' Sería sets up a hypothetical; the infinitive morir is the grammatical subject.

"Paul," I said softly, addressing my brother, "for the first time in my life, I feel nothing for you, nothing for your death; and for the first time I feel everything for you, I feel the pain of your loss as if I had never before known what it was to feel." The vampire addressed the boy. "For the first time I was fully and completely a vampire."
vocabulario
en voz bajasoftly, in a low voice — en voz baja: in a low voice; opposite of en voz alta (aloud)

gramática
  • como si nunca antes hubiera sabido lo que era sentir — Como si + pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo: 'as if he had never before known what it was to feel.' The pluperfect marks the not-knowing as preceding the moment of speaking — a lifetime of not truly feeling, now suddenly reversed.
  • lo que era sentir — Lo que + ser + infinitivo nominalizado: 'what it was to feel.' Lo que era = 'what it was'; the infinitive sentir as the subject of the nominalized expression.
